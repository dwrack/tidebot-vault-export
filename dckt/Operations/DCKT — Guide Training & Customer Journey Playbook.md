# DCKT — Guide Training & Customer Journey Playbook
*The complete experience from first contact to raving fan*

---

## Philosophy

We're not a kayak tour company. We're a memory company. Every person who paddles with us is going to tell someone about it — the only question is what they say and how long it stays with them.

A guide's job has three layers:
1. **Safety** — always first, non-negotiable
2. **Experience** — make the water come alive
3. **Connection** — make the guest feel known, significant, and changed

The best guides understand that the kayak is just the vehicle. The real product is how someone feels when they drive home.

---

## The Full Customer Journey Map

```
DISCOVERY → FIRST CONTACT → BOOKING → PRE-TOUR → ARRIVAL → ON THE WATER → LANDING → POST-TOUR → RETURN
```

Each stage has a job to do. Miss one and the whole chain weakens.

---

## Stage 1 — DISCOVERY
*How they find us*

Most guests come from TripAdvisor, Google, or a friend's recommendation. Your job at this stage: be worth talking about before they've even met you.

**What drives discovery:**
- Reviews (TripAdvisor, Google) — guides create these post-tour
- Social media — Nya handles this, but guides can feed content (photos, moments, stories)
- Word of mouth — the end product of every other stage done right

**Guide role:** Every time you ask for a review or take a great photo of a guest, you're feeding the discovery engine for the next guest.

---

## Stage 2 — FIRST CONTACT
*Call, text, or web inquiry*

This is where most small operators lose people. Phone rings, no one answers, lead goes cold.

**Automated (GHL handles):**
- Missed call → immediate auto-text within 60 seconds
- 3-message lead nurture if no booking within 24–72 hours

**When a guide takes a call or text:**
- Answer in the first ring if possible
- Use their name immediately if it shows in GHL
- Ask: *"Have you been to Door County before?"* and *"Is this a special occasion?"*
- Those two questions unlock everything — you know how to pitch the experience

**The goal of first contact:** Make them feel like they called a friend who happens to run kayak tours, not a business.

---

## Stage 3 — BOOKING
*The transaction that should feel like a celebration*

**Automated (GHL handles):**
- Immediate booking confirmation with personal tone
- Upsell to private tour within 30 minutes if group size fits
- Cross-sell: Lykke Sauna, e-bike rental

**For returning guests (3+ visits):**
GHL flags them. Confirmation message is different:
> *"Welcome back — we've got something a little different planned for you this season."*

**Human layer:**
If it's a large group, a milestone birthday, or a returning guest flagged in the system — someone on the team sends a personal text from David's number. Not automated. Real.

---

## Stage 4 — PRE-TOUR ANTICIPATION
*Building excitement before they arrive*

Most tours start on the water. The best tours start 3 days before.

**Automated sequence (GHL):**

| Message | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| First-timer | T-3 days | Paint the picture of what they're about to see |
| Returning guest | T-3 days | "You've done Cave Point — we're going deeper this time" |
| All guests | T-1 evening | Guide intro by name, what to bring, logistics |
| All guests | Day-of 2hrs before | Energy, confirmation, "guide is on the way" |

**What makes the T-3 message powerful:**
It makes them *feel* the tour before they've taken it. They start imagining the caves, the water, the morning light. By the time they arrive they're already emotionally invested. Excitement converts to enthusiasm converts to a better tour.

**First-timer T-3 example:**
> "Hey [Name]! 3 days until your Cave Point paddle 🌊 Quick fact: those sea caves are only reachable by water. You're about to see something most people in Wisconsin never will. Your guide will take care of everything — no experience needed. Any questions before Saturday?"

**Returning guest T-3 example:**
> "Hey [Name] — you've done this before, so you know what you're in for. This time your guide is going to take you somewhere a little different. We'll talk when you get here. See you Saturday."

## Stage 5 — ARRIVAL
*The first 5 minutes set the tone for everything*

This is the most underdeveloped stage in almost every tour operation. Guests arrive at a parking lot and stand around. That dead time is an opportunity.

**Guide arrival protocol:**
- Be at the launch 20 minutes before guests
- Greet every person by first name if you have the booking list
- For returning guests: acknowledge it immediately
  - *"[Name]! Good to have you back. How many times is this now?"*
  - Let them answer, then: *"Alright — I've got something for you today."*
- For first-timers: ask one genuine question before the safety briefing
  - *"Where are you from? First time in Door County?"*
  - This isn't small talk — it's intel. Now you know how to personalize the next 2 hours.

**The group read:**
In the first 5 minutes, a great guide scans the group:
- Who's nervous? (reassure early, pair in tandem wisely)
- Who's been before? (acknowledge, give them a role — "you've done this, help your partner feel comfortable")
- Who's celebrating something? (find out and make a moment of it on the water)
- Who has a camera out? (they'll be your best social media asset — give them the money shot location)

## Stage 6 — ON THE WATER
*Where the memory gets made*

### For First-Timers

**First 10 minutes:** Confidence-building. They're going to be awkward. Normalize it, celebrate small wins.
> *"You're already paddling better than most people on their first time out."*

**The big reveal moment:** Every tour should have one. A point where you stop paddling, let the kayaks drift, and give them a beat to take it in — and then you *name it*.
> *"Look at where you are right now. You're floating inside a 10,000-year-old sea cave. This rock was being carved by water while humans were still using stone tools. Most people drive past this on Hwy 57 and never see it."*

Silence for 10 seconds. Let it land. That's the photograph their brain takes.

**What to wear / bring reminder:** Already in the bot. But on the water — if they're struggling with sun or forgot something, be the one who has extra sunscreen, an extra hat, a dry bag. Small things that say: *we thought about you.*

---

### For Returning Guests (3+ Visits)

These guests have seen the standard tour. They're back because of you — the place and the people. Give them the **insider layer.**

**The Locals Route:**
Designate one section of water or one landing spot that isn't on the public itinerary. It doesn't have to be dramatic — it just has to feel earned. A quieter cove, a rock ledge you can pull out on, a view from an angle first-timers don't see.

When you take them there: *"This isn't on the website. We don't bring everyone here."*

**The Skill Upgrade:**
> *"You've been out a few times — want me to show you how guides actually paddle? It'll change how you feel on the water."*

Teach one real technique: edging, reading current, entering a cave on the right swell. They leave with a capability they didn't have. That's growth. That sticks.

**The Ecological Reveal:**
Pick one story about this specific stretch of water that most tourists never hear. Rotate it so returning guests hear something new each visit. Options:

- The geology: Silurian dolomite, how the caves formed, how old they actually are
- The shipwrecks: what's below them and visible at certain depths/light
- The invasive species: what the water looked like 40 years ago, what DCKT does about it
- The Indigenous history: who paddled these waters before us and why it mattered to them
- The wildlife: what the herons are doing, what season means for fish movement

Returning guests drive home and tell someone at dinner something they didn't know before. That's the "I'm a little different" moment.

**The Conservation Paddle (for 5x+ guests):**
Optional 30-minute shoreline micro-cleanup before or after the tour. Small bags, pull invasive plants or grab litter along the launch area. Offer it — don't mandate it.

Why it works:
- Activates contribution — the deepest human need
- Creates ownership of the place ("I helped protect this")
- Creates a story worth telling
- Positions DCKT as a values-driven business

## Stage 7 — LANDING
*The close — most guides skip this entirely*

The tour doesn't end when kayaks hit the beach. The last 5 minutes on shore are your highest-leverage moment of the day.

**For all guests:**
Help them out of the kayak, gear off, then before they drift to their car — **pause and connect.**

> *"How was that for you? What was your favorite moment?"*

Let them answer. Don't rush to the next group. This question:
- Creates the memory (articulating the experience cements it neurologically)
- Gives you the quote for a review request
- Tells you what mattered so you can flag it in the CRM

**For first-timers:**
> *"You just did something most people never do. That caves stretch only gets paddled by a few hundred people a year. Hope you come back."*

That's a seed. Water it with the post-tour sequence.

**For returning guests — the annual close:**
> *"You keep coming back — and I love that. Is there something you haven't done yet that you want to do next time? Sunset paddle? Fall colors on the inland route? Tell me now and I'll make a note."*

Then actually log it. Text the team before you leave the launch: *"[Name], 3rd visit, wants sunset tour next time."* Into GHL it goes.

**The photo close:**
Before guests leave — offer to take a group photo. Not just suggest it. *Take the phone and do it.*

> *"Give me your phone — I want to get you all together with the water behind you."*

A great guide-taken photo is more shareable than anything else. It goes on Instagram, it tags DCKT, it does marketing work for months.

## Stage 8 — POST-TOUR
*Automated (GHL) + Human*

**Automated sequence (from SMS Bot SOP):**

| Timing | Message |
|---|---|
| 1 hr after | Personal warmth, reference their experience, photo tag ask |
| 3 hrs after | Review request (TripAdvisor link) with guide name |
| 7 days later | Re-engagement — offer different experience |
| October | End of season, "see you next year" |

**Human layer (guides can do this):**
If a guest had a particularly special moment on the water — an unexpected wildlife sighting, a proposal, a kid who conquered their fear — the guide texts them directly from the DCKT number that same evening.

> *"This is [Guide Name] from today's tour. That moment when [specific thing happened] — I'm still thinking about it. Thank you for being out there with us."*

No call to action. No link. Just a human moment. Those texts get screenshotted and shared.

## Stage 9 — RETURN & ADVOCACY
*The full loop*

**The Paddler's Circle (Returning Guest Program):**

| Visits | Recognition |
|---|---|
| 2nd visit | Guide acknowledges it at arrival + "locals route" access |
| 3rd visit | Personal note or text from David |
| 5th visit | Physical token mailed to them (sticker, patch, branded item) |
| 10th visit | Name on something physical at the launch site |

**The referral ask (from post-tour sequence):**
> *"Know someone who needs a Door County adventure? Share this and they get $10 off their first tour — and we'll send you a thank-you: [link]"*

**The seasonal re-engagement (March/April):**
When bookings open for the new season, returning guests (2+ visits) get a personal message before the general public:
> *"[Name] — season opens in [X] weeks. You've been with us [N] times. You get first pick before we go public. Want me to grab you a date?"*

That's not marketing. That's a relationship.

## Guide Training Checklist

### Before Every Tour
- [ ] Review the booking list — know every guest's name
- [ ] Flag returning guests (2+ visits) in FareHarbor/GHL
- [ ] Note any special occasions (birthday, anniversary, first trip)
- [ ] Check weather window — know the cancellation contingency in advance
- [ ] Prepare your "ecological reveal" for the day

### During Every Tour
- [ ] Greet every guest by name at arrival
- [ ] Returning guests: acknowledge it, signal something different is coming
- [ ] First-timers: ask one genuine question before the briefing
- [ ] Read the group in the first 5 minutes
- [ ] Find the camera person and give them the money shot location
- [ ] Execute the big reveal moment — stop, let it land, name it
- [ ] For returning guests: deliver the locals route + skill upgrade or ecological reveal
- [ ] At landing: ask "what was your favorite moment?"
- [ ] Take the group photo

### After Every Tour
- [ ] Log any notable guest moments in GHL before you leave the launch
- [ ] Log returning guest requests ("wants sunset tour next time")
- [ ] Send personal text for any exceptional moments that day
- [ ] Feed any great photos/stories to Nya for social media

---

## The One Standard

At the end of every tour, a guest should be able to answer yes to:

> *"Did someone here make me feel like I mattered?"*

That's it. That's the job.

Safety keeps people alive. Skills get reviews. But *that feeling* — of being known, of being taken somewhere real, of having someone care — that's what brings them back. That's what they tell their friends. That's what makes this business something worth building.
