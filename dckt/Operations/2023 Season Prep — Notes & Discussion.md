## Context: 2023 Season Prep (Inferred from Other Sources)

The following are informed by the 2021 Weekly Reports, the Customer Feedback dataset (2019–2024), and known DCKT operational patterns. These represent likely 2023 prep topics:

### Guide Management
- **Hiring timeline:** Guides typically start coming on in April/May; need enough staff by Memorial Day weekend
- **Training gaps identified in customer feedback:** Pacing (guides going too far ahead), paddling instruction for beginners, cave availability communication, cliff jump communication
- **Guide quality variance:** Named guides praised repeatedly (Dave, Bill, Becca, Erin, Rachel); named guides with complaints (Hannah, Justin, specific unnamed)
- **2021 lesson:** When staff leaves at end of season, run limited schedule (9am, 11am, 1pm slots only)

### Tour Operations
- **Highest complaint trigger:** Advertising caves that customers can't access due to weather/water conditions — needs upfront communication protocol
- **Cliff jumping** — repeatedly promised but sometimes skipped; needs clear guide decision framework
- **Tandem kayak transparency** — not disclosed at booking; repeated complaint 2019–2023; needs website fix
- **Group size control** — 20+ person groups with one guide created safety incidents in 2021; cap and ratio policy needed
- **Beginner handling** — no experience screening at booking causes group delays and safety issues; consider experience-level questions at booking

### Equipment & Facilities
- **Kayak seat backs** breaking (multiple complaints); pre-season equipment audit needed
- **Cracked kayaks** taking on water (reported 2019–2020)
- **Life jackets with body odor** (reported 2021) — wash/replace protocol needed
- **Paddle drip guards missing** (first complaint 2019)
- **Payment system** — complaints about manual CC entry; upgrade to tap/Apple Pay needed

### Booking & Communication
- **Weather cancellation protocol** was a major failure point 2022–2023: customers showed up to cancelled tours with no notification
- **Email/text confirmation** system for cancellations must be proactive — same-day morning notification minimum
- **Pricing transparency** — multiple complaints about unexpected charges, price mismatches between website and actual billing
- **Photo delivery** — promised at booking, not always delivered; photos uploaded late

### E-Bike / Alternate Activity Operations
- Bike tours were a growing revenue stream by 2021
- **E-bike complaints:** poor instruction on hills/speed, riding on highways (felt unsafe), bikes in poor condition (loose seats, low charge, broken chains)
- When weather cancels kayak tours, bike tours are offered as substitute — pricing confusion about cost difference needs addressing
