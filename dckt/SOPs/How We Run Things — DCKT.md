# How We Run Things
### Door County Kayak Tours

Read this before your first day. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask your manager to walk you through it or check the Slack channel.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

Door County is your territory. You know the water, you know the weather, you know the regulars who come back every summer. You don't wait for someone in Portland to tell you what to do.

If a guest asks about the best place to eat after the tour, you have an answer. If the wind shifts and the route needs to change, you make the call. If something at the shop needs fixing, you fix it or flag it same day.

You're not just a guide filling a slot. You're running a piece of this business every time you're on the water.

---

## 2. Be Real

We don't do scripted tours or canned small talk. You talk from what you actually know about Door County, the water, the geology, the wildlife. If a guest asks something you don't know, say "I don't know, but I'll find out" instead of making something up.

Same goes for how we talk to each other. If something's off, say so. Don't perform enthusiasm you don't feel. Honest and direct beats polished and hollow.

Our brand has personality because our people do. Bring yours.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

This is the line. Show up prepared. Follow through on what you said you'd do. Don't let problems sit because the conversation is uncomfortable.

We give real grace. Everyone has rough days, personal stuff, slow weeks. That's human. But there's a difference between struggling and checking out. If you've lost the thread, say something. We'd rather work it out than watch you go through the motions.

Respect also means respecting each other's time. If you're late, if you leave gear dirty, if you skip a step because "it's fine," that falls on the person after you.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If you figured out a better way to load the van, share it. If a guest asked a question you didn't have a good answer for, drop it in the channel so we can all get better.

Write it down, send a voice memo, post a photo. The format doesn't matter. What matters is it doesn't disappear when your shift ends.

The SOPs, the Slack channels, the checklists: these exist so a new guide in June can learn from what you figured out in May. Build the thing you wish you'd had on your first day.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

Waivers get signed on every tour. No exceptions. Gear gets checked before every outing. Life jackets are inspected, not assumed. If conditions aren't safe, we cancel. We don't hedge it.

Skipping a safety step because you're running late or the group seems experienced is a termination conversation. Not a warning. Not a coaching moment.

If you see something unsafe, say it immediately. If you're not sure whether something is a safety issue, treat it like one until you know. There is no version of this job where cutting a safety corner is worth it.

---

## The Deal

These five things aren't aspirational. They're the floor. You can be great at this job in a hundred different ways, and we want you to bring whatever makes you you. But these five are non-negotiable.

If this sounds like the kind of place you want to spend a summer, you're going to love it here.
