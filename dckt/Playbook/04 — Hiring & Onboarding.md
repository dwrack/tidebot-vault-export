# 04 — Hiring & Onboarding
*Part of [[DCKT Playbook — Master Index]]*

---

## Hiring Overview

DCKT typically hires **8–14 staff** per season across three roles. Hiring starts in January with a goal of having the full team committed by late January / early February. Six or more are typically return employees.

---

## Positions

| Role | Count | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Kayak Guide / Driver** | 6–10 | Valid driver's license, physically capable (lift 50 lb kayak), public speaking comfort |
| **Receptionist / Reception** | 1–2 | Phone skills, FareHarbor competence, calm under pressure |
| **Social Media / Comms** | 1 | Content creation, Instagram/Facebook/TikTok |
| **Assistant Manager** | 1 | Guide experience + management interest, can stay through September |

---

## Where Applications Come In

Applications are submitted via a **FareHarbor form** and forwarded to: doorcountykayaking@gmail.com

**Application fields:**
- Name, DOB, Driver's License #
- Email, Phone, Address
- Positions applying for
- Passions in life
- Certifications or notable accomplishments
- First day / last day available + any vacation needs
- Plans for other jobs / desired hours per week
- Housing in Door County (or do they need it provided)
- Largest group spoken to / organized / directed
- 5-year vision
- Physical ability (can you lift 50 lbs)
- Attitude question (e.g., "If you cleaned bathrooms for a week, would you complain?")

**What to look for:**
- Guides that can stay May through September minimum
- People who lean into adventure, education, and public engagement
- Return employees: send personal invite letters before public posting
- "Quirky is fine — zero effort on the application is a red flag" (David's standard)

## Hiring Timeline

| Date | Action |
|---|---|

---

## Guide Invite Letter

Returning guides receive a personal invite letter each year. Key elements:
- Warm welcome back ("Mandi Schuyler is the guide name you can send an invite letter to")
- Training is waived for returning guides — they do not need to pay for the ACA training workshop
- Confirm availability, start/end dates, and housing needs

---

## Housing

- DCKT operates a **staff house** at/near the Jacksonport HQ
- Rate: **$550/month** for a single room (2026 rate — per Bella's contract)
- Housing is tied to full-time employment — it is not independent
- Guides sign a **housing/lease agreement** with basic rules: yard maintenance, cleaning, living respectfully with roommates
- Housing agreement drafted/updated in March each year (see Drive)

---

## Application Screening Checklist

Before scheduling an interview:

- [ ] Do they have a valid driver's license?
- [ ] Can they be there May through at least Labor Day?
- [ ] Do they need housing — and can we accommodate?
- [ ] Do they have any group/public speaking experience?
- [ ] Did they put actual thought into the application? (minimum bar)

---

## Interview Process

1. Phone screen or in-person interview (March–April)
2. Follow-up interview for strong candidates (assistant manager track)
3. David or manager reviews all applications
4. Guide invite letter sent to confirmed hires
5. Housing agreement sent if applicable
6. Confirm start date and training week attendance

**Interview note from David (2024):** "Worth a quick interview for quirky/energetic types even if the application is low-effort. Bartending competition winner + hostel front desk + dance captain = actually solid tour guide experience."

## ACA Certification

- **American Canoe Association (ACA)** certification is the national standard for kayak guides
- Return employees with ACA certification: waive training cost
- New employees: budget for ACA workshop pre-season
- Track expiration dates for all certifications
- See: [[Operations/ACA Guide Certification]]

## Discipline Policy (from Employee Manual)

1. **First infraction:** Scrub all equipment. Re-read employee manual.
2. **Second infraction:** Management review. Bowl patrol for the season.
3. **Third infraction:** Terminated.

Rules that trigger discipline:
- Discussing alcohol/drugs with guests
- Swearing, sexual comments, or inappropriate jokes
- Accidents attributable to hangovers
- No-shows / repeated tardiness

---

## What "Good" Looks Like

From 20 years of hiring: the best DCKT guides share these traits:
- **Confidence** — calm under stress, comfortable in front of groups
- **Accountability** — they own mistakes and fix them
- **Flexibility** — weather cancels tours; good guides adapt without drama
- **Friendliness** — not performed, genuine
- **Physical endurance** — full days on water + loading trailers

The job is part outdoor educator, part entertainer, part safety officer. Hire for personality first, technique second.
