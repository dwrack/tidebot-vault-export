# DCKT — Offer Architecture & Pricing
*Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers framework applied to Door County Kayak Tours*

---

## The Core Principle (Hormozi)

**A grand slam offer** is one where the value is so obviously higher than the price that saying "no" feels irrational.

The goal isn't to raise prices. It's to make the existing price feel like a steal by clearly communicating what's included, stacking perceived value, and removing every reason to hesitate.

---

## The Value Stack (Cave Point Kayak Tour)

**What you actually deliver:**
> *When you book a DCKT Cave Point Kayak Tour, you get:*
>
> ✅ **Expert local guide** — someone who knows every cave, every route, and the story of this shoreline — **Comparable value: $75 private guide fee**
> ✅ **All gear provided** — kayak, paddle, PFD, dry bag, spray skirt — **Comparable value: $80 rental**
> ✅ **Safety instruction + technique coaching** — from certified guides — **Comparable value: $50 beginner lesson**
> ✅ **Curated route to spots most visitors never find** — only accessible by water — **Value: exclusive**
> ✅ **Weather-flexible booking** — cancel or reschedule with confidence — **Value: peace of mind**
> ✅ **Post-tour local guide** — restaurants, hikes, hidden gems from people who live here — **Value: $20**
> ✅ **Group photos on the water** — memories you'll actually keep — **Comparable value: $50**
>
> **Total comparable value: $275+**
> **Your investment: $59 per person**

This framing should be on the website, in the chatbot, and in any conversation where someone asks "is it worth it?"

## Objection Removal

Every hesitation has a counter. These should be in the website, chatbot, and guide's vocabulary:

| Objection | Counter |
|---|---|
| "I've never kayaked before" | "That's our favorite kind of guest. No experience needed — we'll have you paddling confidently in 10 minutes." |
| "Is it safe for kids?" | "We take kids as young as 5. Our guides are trained specifically for mixed-ability groups." |
| "What if the weather is bad?" | "If we cancel for weather, your booking is fully protected. No hassle, no fees — reschedule anytime." |
| "Is it worth $59?" | [Value stack above] |
| "We only have a few hours" | "The tour is 2.5 hours total — that's the whole experience. No wasted time." |
| "We're not that athletic" | "This is not a workout. This is floating through sea caves. Any fitness level is welcome." |
| "We already did it last year" | "Have you done the Sunset Tour? Completely different experience — most guests say it's their favorite." |

## Pricing Psychology (Dan Kennedy)

**Anchor high, land low:**
Lead with the value stack, then reveal the price. $59 feels different after you've framed it against $275 in comparable value.

**Specificity:**
"$59 per person" beats "affordable tours." "4.9 stars from 847 reviews" beats "top-rated."

**Urgency without desperation:**
"Only 3 spots left this Saturday" (when true) is powerful. Use it honestly. GHL can automate this based on FareHarbor availability.

**Scarcity is real:**
DCKT has a finite number of guides and kayaks per day. This is genuine scarcity — lean into it.

## The Gift Card Strategy

Gift cards are the highest-margin product DCKT has:
- Zero labor cost at point of sale
- ~20–30% of gift cards are never redeemed (float)
- The recipient is a new customer DCKT otherwise might not reach
- Perfect for holidays, birthdays, "what do I get the person who has everything"

**Push points:**
- Holiday campaign (November–December)
- Post-tour ask: "Want to give this experience to someone?"
- On the website prominently — not buried
- In the guide's send-off: "We sell gift cards if you want to bring someone next time"

## Corporate / Group Events (Untapped)

DCKT is sitting on a corporate team-building gold mine. Companies pay $100–$200/person for guided outdoor experiences. Current tours at $59 are underpriced for this market.

**What to build:**
- "DCKT Team Experience" package — private guide(s), customizable route, post-tour gathering spot
- Price: $125–$175/person minimum, 10-person minimum
- Add-ons: catered lunch on shore, Lykke Sauna for the group, e-bikes
- Market to: Chicago companies with Door County retreats, Wisconsin-based companies, HR/events coordinators

**Who to contact:** Door County Chamber of Commerce, local event venues, team-building directories
