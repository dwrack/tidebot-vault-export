# DCKT — Full Customer Journey Map
*Every touchpoint from first awareness to lifelong advocate*
*What the customer feels · What we do · What the ideal outcome is*

---

## The Journey at a Glance

```
AWARENESS → CONSIDERATION → BOOKING → ANTICIPATION → ARRIVAL → ON-WATER → SEND-OFF → POST-TOUR → RETURN → ADVOCATE
```

---

## Stage 1 — AWARENESS
*They don't know us yet*

**What the customer feels:** Curious, planning, overwhelmed by options, maybe skeptical ("is this worth it?")

**How they find us:**
- Google: "things to do door county" / "kayak tours door county"
- TripAdvisor / Yelp / Viator listing
- Instagram — someone they follow tagged us or we showed up in Explore
- Friend referral — "you have to do this"
- Drove by the launch site, saw the blue van

**What we do:**
- SEO-optimized website with StoryBrand messaging ("We take you to the hidden side of Door County")
- Google Ads targeting "door county kayak tours" and variations
- 4.9 stars visible everywhere — Google, TripAdvisor, social
- Instagram presence that shows the experience visually — caves, water, people's faces
- Social proof throughout: "800+ five-star reviews" on every page

**Ideal outcome:** They click through to the website or call number with genuine curiosity and intent to book.

**Failure mode:** They bounce because the website doesn't immediately answer "is this right for me?"

---

## Stage 2 — CONSIDERATION
*They're interested but haven't committed*

**What the customer feels:** Excited but uncertain. "Will I be able to do this?" "Is it worth the money?" "What if the weather is bad?"

**Objections to address:**
- "I've never kayaked before" → "That's our favorite kind of guest"
- "What if it rains?" → Clear weather policy, easy rebooking
- "Is it safe for kids/seniors/beginners?" → Social proof from those exact guests
- "Is it worth $59?" → Value stack makes it feel like a deal

**What we do:**
- Website FAQ answers every objection directly
- Chatbot handles real-time questions (Rawgrowth.ai install)
- Missed call auto-reply + 3-message nurture sequence (GHL) — see [[Playbook/04 — GHL Automation Sequences]]
- "No experience needed" messaging everywhere
- Reviews from first-timers, families, seniors visible on site

**Ideal outcome:** They book. Or they respond to a nurture message and book.

**Failure mode:** They leave the website to "think about it" and never come back.

---

## Stage 3 — THE BOOKING MOMENT
*They just said yes*

**What the customer feels:** Excited, maybe a little relieved the decision is made, hoping they made the right choice

**What we do:**
- FareHarbor confirmation → triggers GHL workflow immediately
- Booking celebration message: *"🎉 You're on! [Name], we just confirmed your Cave Point Tour on [DATE] — we are SO excited to have you."*
- "Special occasion?" question — opens upsell conversation naturally
- Private tour upgrade offer (30 min after booking)
- Cross-sell: e-bike rental, Lykke Sauna

**What Bella owns:** Monitoring for upsell responses, handling any manual follow-up

**Ideal outcome:** They feel like the decision was celebrated, not just processed. Optional: they upgrade or add on.

**Failure mode:** They book and hear nothing until the day-before reminder. The excitement fades.

---

## Stage 4 — ANTICIPATION ARC
*Between booking and tour (T-3 days to day-of)*

**What the customer feels:** Anticipation building, starting to think about what to pack, maybe talking about it with whoever they're bringing

**What we do (GHL automated):**

> **T-3 days:**
> "Hey [Name]! 3 days until your Cave Point kayak tour 🌊 Did you know the sea caves there are only accessible by water? You're about to see something most people never get to experience up close. Any questions before [DAY]?"

> **T-1 day (evening):**
> "Tomorrow's the day! Your guide [GUIDE NAME] is stoked to take you out.
> Quick checklist: ✅ Hat + sunscreen ✅ Light layers (no cotton) ✅ Water shoes ✅ Camera — trust us 📸
> Meeting point: Schauer Park Boat Launch at [TIME]."

> **Day-of (2 hours before):**
> "Good morning [Name]! Today's the day 🙌 Conditions look [great]. [Guide Name] is heading to the launch now. See you at [TIME] — look for the blue van!"

**Ideal outcome:** They arrive already emotionally invested, excited, and prepared. No first-timer confusion about where to go or what to bring.

**Failure mode:** They forget. They show up underprepared. They're in a neutral or stressed state when they arrive.

---

## Stage 5 — ARRIVAL
*First 5 minutes on-site*

**What the customer feels:** Transition mode — leaving their day behind, entering something new. Slightly uncertain.

**What we do:**
- Guide moves toward them immediately — never waits
- Name used within the first 30 seconds
- 10-second orientation given right away (removes uncertainty)
- Energy is warm, confident, unhurried
- Special occasion acknowledged if noted in booking

**The Ritz-Carlton standard:** guests feel *expected and wanted.* Not processed.

**Ideal outcome:** Within 5 minutes, they've released the stress of their day and are fully present.

**Failure mode:** They stand around wondering what's happening. First impression is disorganized.

---

## Stage 6 — THE SAFETY BRIEFING
*Necessary friction, handled with skill*

**What the customer feels:** Slightly impatient to get going, possibly worried about a lot of rules

**What we do:**
- Briefing starts with stakes, not rules ("let's make sure you have an amazing time")
- Non-negotiables delivered with personality, not recitation
- 5–7 minutes total — efficient and human
- Closes with anticipation ("any questions? Let's go — I want to show you something")

**Ideal outcome:** Everyone is safe and informed, and the energy is higher after the briefing than before.

**Failure mode:** Energy tanks. Guests are bored or scared. The tour starts in a hole.

---

## Stage 7 — ON THE WATER
*The experience itself — 1.5–2 hours*

**What the customer feels:** A spectrum: wonder, calm, exhilaration, awe, presence, disconnection from daily life

**What we do:**
- Guide calibrates to group in first 10 minutes
- Struggling/nervous guests supported privately, not publicly
- Minimum 3 "named moments" — guide calls out what's beautiful and why it's rare
- 1–2 pieces of genuinely interesting place knowledge delivered conversationally
- Returning guests get something different (locals route, skill upgrade)
- Pace, conversation, and route adjusted in real time to group's energy

**The Robbins principle:** people are in a peak state on the water. This is when the transformation happens. The guide's job is to deepen it, not interrupt it.

**Ideal outcome:** At least one moment where the group goes quiet because what they're seeing is too beautiful for words. They will remember this.

**Failure mode:** The guide talks too much, the route is uninspired, guests are checking the time.

---

## Stage 8 — THE SEND-OFF
*Last 5 minutes — the lasting impression*

**What the customer feels:** The warm glow of something good just happened. Slightly sad it's over.

**What we do:**
- Let them own the accomplishment: "First time kayaking and you just did Cave Point. How does that feel?"
- Plant the seed for return: "You haven't seen the sunset tour yet — completely different planet"
- Returning guest close: "What do you want to do next time?" — logged in CRM
- Mention photos: "We'll text those over — tag us if you post"
- Personal review ask (in person, not just automated text)

**Ideal outcome:** They leave with a plan to come back and a reason to tell someone about today before they even get to the car.

**Failure mode:** Guide is already mentally on to the next group. Guests drift to their car. The relationship ends.

---

## Stage 9 — POST-TOUR SEQUENCE
*1 hour to 7 days after*

**What the customer feels:** The afterglow. Sharing the experience. Telling people about it.

**What we do (GHL automated):**

> **1 hour after tour:**
> "That was incredible, [Name]! It was so great having you with us today 🌊 If you captured any shots, tag us — we'd love to share them! @doorcountykayak"

> **3 hours after (review ask):**
> "Hey [Name] — one small favor? If [Guide Name] and the crew made your day, a quick Google or TripAdvisor review means the world to our small team: [LINK] 🙏"

> **24 hours (referral ask):**
> "If any friends or family are coming to Door County this summer, we'd love to take care of them. Share this and they'll get $10 off — and we'll send you a thank-you too: [LINK]"

> **7 days (re-engagement):**
> "Hey [Name]! You did Cave Point — have you ever done the Sunset Tour? Completely different vibe, totally worth coming back for. Reply 'sunset' and we'll check availability."

**Ideal outcome:** Review posted. Referral sent. Return visit booked or intent established.

**Failure mode:** The relationship ends with the review ask. 24,000 past customers sitting dormant.

---

## Stage 10 — THE RETURN
*2nd visit and beyond*

**What the customer feels:** Familiar excitement, wanting more, feeling like they have a place here

**What we do:**
- Returning guest flag in FareHarbor → different GHL sequence
- Guide briefed on their history before arrival
- "Welcome back" — used within first 30 seconds
- New experience delivered — locals route, skill upgrade, different story
- Alumni milestone hit if applicable (3rd visit = personal note, 5th = token, 10th = recognition at launch)

**Ideal outcome:** They feel *known.* They tell people "we have our spot in Door County."

**Failure mode:** They're treated like strangers. The second visit feels the same as the first. There's no reason to keep coming back.

---

## Stage 11 — THE ADVOCATE
*The goal of the entire journey*

**What the customer feels:** Ownership. Pride. Connection to the place and the brand.

**What they do:**
- Tell friends unprompted ("you HAVE to do this")
- Post on social with tag
- Leave reviews on multiple platforms
- Buy gift cards for others
- Return every year
- Become part of Paddler's Circle

**What we do:**
- Referral program: $10 off for their referral, thank-you for them
- Paddler's Circle: recognizes and rewards the most loyal guests
- Social sharing: repost their photos, name them, make them visible
- Gift cards: easiest entry point for advocates to give the experience to others

**Ideal outcome:** Every advocate generates 2–3 new customers per year. At 100 advocates, that's 200–300 new bookings from zero ad spend.

## The Journey at a Glance — Emotion Map

| Stage | Customer Emotion | Our Job |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Curious, searching | Stand out. Be findable. Be compelling. |
| Consideration | Uncertain, evaluating | Remove doubt. Make "yes" easy. |
| Booking | Excited, relieved | Celebrate the decision. Plant anticipation. |
| Anticipation | Building excitement | Feed the excitement. Prepare without boring. |
| Arrival | Transitioning, uncertain | Make them feel expected and welcomed. |
| Briefing | Impatient, slightly anxious | Keep it human. Build confidence. |
| On water | Wonder, presence, aliveness | Deepen the experience. Name the moments. |
| Send-off | Warm, satisfied, slightly sad | Close with intention. Plant the return. |
| Post-tour | Glowing, sharing | Capture the glow. Ask for reviews + referrals. |
| Return | Familiar, wanting more | Show them something new. Make them feel known. |
| Advocate | Proud, connected, ownership | Recognize and reward. Give them something to share. |
