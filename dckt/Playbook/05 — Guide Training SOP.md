## Training Structure

**Week 1 (early arrivals):** Main tours — 2-hour Cave Point, Door Bluff, E-bikes
**Week 2 (new arrivals):** Shadow training — new guides shadow experienced guides for full tours
**FareHarbor/reception training:** All staff, before season opens

Return employees: skip shadow training. Confirm they've reviewed any manual updates.

## Guide Required Gear (On-Water)

Every guide must have the following on every tour:

**Guide Bag:**
- 2 extra waters + granola bar (emergency fuel / diabetic guests)
- Correct-length tow rope (shortened with daisy chain)
- Carabiner
- Multitool
- Cell phone with waterproof case
- Flares
- VHF Radio (mandatory — see below)
- Whistle (mandatory)
- Extra paddle (mandatory as of 2025 manual update)
- Additional water bottles for guest dehydration emergencies

**Seasonal additions (fall + early spring):**
- Wind breaker / rain jacket
- Blanket / towel (hypothermia risk)
- Sweatshirt

**On-Water First Aid Kit:**
- Tweezers, Dramamine/crystallized ginger
- Alcohol wipes, Advil
- Gauze, band-aids, waterproof bandage glue
- Insect sting relief / repellent, sunscreen

**Vehicle First Aid Kit:**
- OSHA 106-piece 25-person bulk kit — located behind driver's seat on E-350 and E-450
- Inspect and restock at season start

## VHF Radio Protocol

| Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **16** | Emergency / Coast Guard distress |
| **3** | Call channel between guides |
| **4** | Weather |

- VHF radios are expensive — always secure with carabiner to bag or PFD, never just clipped loosely
- If not charging: check for corrosion on contacts
- Ensure both radios on the same channel letter (U/I/C) — hold CH/WX to switch

**Water signals (backup):**
- Vertical paddle, static = come to me
- Horizontal paddle = stop
- Vertical paddle, waving = distress / help

## Weather Decision Matrix

Check **3 weather sources** before every tour:
1. NOAA.gov (marine conditions)
2. WeatherUnderground.com
3. iKitesurf.com

Check locations: Sturgeon Bay, Sister Bay, and Northport Pier. NOAA text = marine conditions. Picture forecast = land conditions. **Keep checking as tours arrive — forecast changes.**

**Ask other guides and photographer for on-water conditions at caves.**

| Wind Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| S/SE/SW < 10mph | Paddle anywhere |
| S/SE/SW 10–20mph | Bayside (Door Bluff) — stay close to shore |
| S/SE/SW 15–20mph | Cancel unless: athletic, experienced, no small kids, not large group |
| S/SE/SW 15–25mph | Cancel tour |
| W wind 0–20mph | Lakeside (Cave Point) — stay close to shore 10–20mph |
| NW wind 0–20mph | Lakeside — downwinder Cave Point → Whitefish Bay Boat Launch at 10–20mph |
| N wind 0–20mph | Lakeside — downwinder Cave Point → Whitefish at 10–20mph |
| NE wind 10–20mph | Athletic beginners only — do NOT launch boats in morning, wait for calm |
| NE wind 15–25mph | Cancel tours |
| E wind (rare) | Usually paddle anywhere |

**Guide decides location ~20 minutes before scheduled departure.** NEVER guarantee a location in advance.

## Safety Briefing Script (Key Points)

1. Bathroom before departure — there may not be an opportunity on the water
2. Tour is "challenge by choice" — if they want to stop, they must speak up
3. PFDs are required at all times on water
4. No drugs or alcohol
5. Sunscreen and water highly recommended
6. Show paddling location on Door County map
7. PFD fitting — loosen straps, cinch down, check every guest
   - Child PFD: max 90 lbs
8. Waiver via QR code / iWaivers — all guests must sign

**Safety video link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q4nUBYtx0No&t=29s

## Paddling Lesson (Pre-Launch)

1. Sit up straight, centered — shoulders relaxed, hips loose
2. Larger/more confident paddler in back (controls direction)
3. Front person sets cadence; back person follows
4. Paddle signals: stop (horizontal), come to me (vertical static), distress (vertical waving)
5. Stability check at launch — show how much movement they have
6. Eyes straight ahead, look with head not body
7. Max group: **14 guests per guide.** For groups larger than 14 (single party): 2 guides + both vans.

---

## Capsize Recovery Protocol

1. Alert the group — have others take shelter or hold position
2. Paddle up quickly and confidently — especially in cold water
3. "Are you OK?" — assess first
4. Sit-on-top recovery:
   - Guest holds kayak safely
   - Roll kayak upright, pull parallel to your boat
   - Raft up — secure fore cockpit
   - Most agile person re-enters first, then stabilizes while second climbs in
5. Set up tow if needed — advise group beforehand that towing may occur
   - Present tow as "temporary boost" — guest still paddles
   - Towing >50% of tour = **20% automatic gratuity** (notify guests before tour)

## Towing Policy

## Trailer Operation

**Attach trailer:**
1. Remove hitch pin
2. Pull hitch lever upright
3. Place 2" hitch receiver over 2" ball
4. Push lever down
5. Replace hitch pin
6. Connect safety chains + trailer lights to van
7. Pull up trailer wheel (pin out, slide up toward rear)
8. Double-check all connections

**Load boats:**
- 1 kayak: upside down
- 2–3 kayaks: sideways, facing in

## Talking Points by Location

See [[Operations/DCKT — Guide Training & Customer Journey Playbook]] for full ecological/geological talking points.

**Quick reference:**
- **Cave Point:** 420 million year old Silurian dolomite, Niagara Escarpment, cave formation from wave action (not karst), shipwreck of Australasia (1896)
- **Door Bluff:** Niagara Escarpment (same formation), Eagle Tower history, How Door County got its name — Porte des Morts / Death's Door, Potawatomi vs Winnebago battle
- **Eco Estuary:** 40% of Wisconsin wetlands lost, UNESCO Ramsar Convention designation, Hines emerald dragonfly nymphs, boreal forest, invasive species
- **General Door County:** 300+ miles of shoreline, Formation of interior lakes (Clark Lake, Europe Lake, Kangaroo Lake)
