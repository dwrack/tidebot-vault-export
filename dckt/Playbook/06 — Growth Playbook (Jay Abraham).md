# DCKT — Growth Playbook
*Jay Abraham's 3 Ways to Grow, applied to Door County Kayak Tours*
*Plus: Dan Kennedy's reactivation strategy for 24,000 dormant contacts*

---

## The Framework

Jay Abraham's core principle: **there are only 3 ways to grow any business.**

1. **More customers** — increase the number of people who buy from you
2. **More frequency** — get existing customers to buy more often
3. **More value per transaction** — increase what each customer spends

Most businesses obsess over #1 and ignore #2 and #3. But #2 and #3 cost almost nothing and compound fast.

---

## Way 1 — More Customers

### A. Referral Engine (Highest ROI)
Your best customers are your best salespeople. They just need to be asked and given a reason.

**What to build:**
- Automated referral ask in post-tour sequence (GHL Sequence 4, Step 3)
- Offer: $10 off for the referred friend, small thank-you for the referrer
- Landing page: dedicated referral URL that tracks source
- Guide verbal ask at send-off: plant it in person, reinforce with the automated text

**Math:** If 10% of 1,000 annual guests refer one person, that's 100 new customers from zero ad spend.

### B. Reactivate 24,000 Past Contacts (Dan Kennedy Win-Back)
You have 24,084 unique contacts. Most haven't been contacted in years. Some of them are sitting in Wisconsin or Illinois right now, thinking about what to do in Door County this summer.

**The 3-message win-back sequence:**

> **Email 1 — "It's been a while":**
> Subject: "It's been a minute, [Name]"
> "Hey [Name] — it's David from Door County Kayak Tours. You came out with us [year] and I hope it was as good as I remember. We've made some changes since then — new guides, new routes, and honestly a better experience all around. If Door County is on your radar this summer, we'd love to have you back. Here's $10 off your next tour: [CODE] — just because. Hope to see you on the water."

> **Email 2 — Social proof (if no open/click, +7 days):**
> Subject: "847 people can't be wrong"
> "Hey [Name] — quick follow-up. We're sitting at 4.9 stars across 847 reviews this season. People keep saying the same thing: 'I wish I'd done this sooner.' If Door County is in your plans this summer, spots are already filling. [BOOK NOW LINK]"

> **Email 3 — Last chance (if still no response, +7 days):**
> Subject: "Last one from us, [Name]"
> "We won't keep bugging you — just wanted to make sure you saw the $10 off before it expires [DATE]. If the timing isn't right, no worries. We'll be here when Door County calls. [BOOK NOW]"

**Expected results:** 2–5% reactivation rate on a warm list = 480–1,200 bookings from people who already know and trusted you.

### C. Google Ads Optimization
Already running. Opportunities:
- Ensure ads appear for "door county things to do," "cave point kayak," "door county family activities"
- Retargeting: anyone who visited doorcountykayaktours.com but didn't book gets ads for 30 days
- Seasonal bid adjustments: increase in May–August, reduce September–October

### D. TripAdvisor + Viator Optimization
- Respond to every review (shows responsiveness, improves algorithm ranking)
- Add new photos every season (drives clicks)
- Keep availability updated — empty availability slots lose bookings

### E. Local Partnerships
- Door County hotels + B&Bs: rack cards, preferred vendor status, concierge referrals
- Door County restaurants: reciprocal recommendations
- Airbnb hosts in the area: "things to do" recommendations
- Door County Visitor Bureau: featured listing, co-marketing

## Way 2 — More Frequency

### A. Multiple Tour Products
The customer who did Cave Point should have a clear next step:
- Sunset Tour ("completely different vibe — see the peninsula turn gold")
- Inland route ("we never take first-timers here — this one's for people who know what they're doing")
- Fall colors paddle (September) — undermarketed, stunning, drives repeat visits
- Family specialty tour (different pacing and content for kid-focused groups)

**Build:** distinct FareHarbor listings for each experience, with distinct GHL re-engagement messages that reference what they've already done.

### B. Seasonal Rhythm
Returning guests think in seasons. Build a calendar they can plan around:

| Season | Hook |
|---|---|
| Memorial Day | "The season opens — get the best dates before they're gone" |
| Summer | Peak season, scarcity messaging, sunset tours |
| Labor Day | "Last big weekend — don't miss it" |
| September | "Fall colors paddle — the most underrated thing in Door County" |
| October | "Season close — one last time on the water" |
| Winter | Gift card push — "give someone an adventure" |
| March | Season opening — early booking priority for past guests |

### C. Paddler's Circle (Loyalty)
The full loyalty program spec is in [[Playbook/08 — Paddler's Circle Loyalty Program]].
Core mechanic: each visit earns a milestone. Milestones earn recognition, rewards, and identity.

### D. Cross-Sell to Other DCKT Experiences
- E-bike rental: before or after a kayak tour, different perspective on the same peninsula
- Lykke Sauna: perfect pairing, warm-down experience, date night add-on
- Cave Point Place (lodging): stay, kayak, sauna — the Door County package

## Way 3 — More Value Per Transaction

### A. Private Tour Upgrade
The single highest-leverage upsell. For a group of 4–6, the price difference between shared and private is often $50–$100 per person — but the experience gap is enormous.

**Sell it:** at booking, 30 min after booking, during post-tour re-engagement for next visit.

**Frame it:** "For [their group size], the math actually makes private worth it — you get the whole experience to yourselves for [X more per person]."

### B. Bundle Packaging
The kayak + sauna combo is the most natural pairing in the portfolio. Sell it as a named package:

**The Door County Day Package:**
> Morning: Cave Point Kayak Tour (2.5 hrs)
> Afternoon: Lykke Sauna session (2 hrs)
> Price: $X per person (slight savings vs. booking separately)

Market to: couples, birthday groups, bachelorette parties, anyone who has a full day in Door County

### C. Corporate/Group Premium
See [[Playbook/05 — Offer Architecture & Pricing]] — corporate events are priced at 2–3x the standard tour rate and represent a completely untapped revenue stream.

### D. Gift Cards (Deferred Revenue)
Every gift card sold is revenue today for a service delivered later (or never, if unredeemed). Push them hard at the end of season and during holidays.

## The Math — What This Looks Like

**Current state (estimated):**
- ~1,000 bookings/year
- Average transaction: $65 (mix of solo + group)
- Annual revenue: ~$65,000 from tours

**With these levers:**
| Lever | Impact |
|---|---|
| Reactivate 3% of 24k contacts | +720 bookings |
| Referral program (10% of guests refer 1) | +100 bookings |
| Upsell 15% to private tour (+$80/person avg) | +$12,000 revenue |
| Add $20 avg cross-sell (e-bike, sauna) | +$20,000 revenue |
| Corporate events (10 events × 15 people × $150) | +$22,500 revenue |
| Gift cards (200 × $75 avg) | +$15,000 revenue |

**Conservative scenario:** +$85,000 additional revenue with the same number of actual tours.
