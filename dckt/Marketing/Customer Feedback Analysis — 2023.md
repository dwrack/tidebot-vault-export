# Customer Feedback Analysis — 2023

**Sources:**
- Door County Kayak Tours Feedback (Google Form Responses) — 2019 through 2024
- TripAdvisor Reviews (via Alex Mixx research spreadsheet) — 25 reviews collected

**Date of Analysis:** March 29, 2026
**Total Feedback Entries:** ~150+ form responses across 2019–2024 (all negative/constructive — this is a complaints form)
**TripAdvisor Reviews:** 25 curated positive reviews for marketing use

---

## Important Framing Note

The customer feedback spreadsheet is a **complaints and constructive feedback form** — customers who filled it out were prompted when something went wrong. This is NOT a representative sample of all customers. The vast majority of DCKT customers likely had great experiences (as evidenced by the TripAdvisor reviews). Analyze the patterns as operational signals, not as a verdict on the overall customer experience.

---

## Top Complaint Themes (Ranked by Frequency)

### 1. Cave Access — Most Common Single Complaint
**Frequency:** Appears in at least 40–50% of all kayak tour complaints

Customers consistently booked the "cave tour" expecting to paddle inside caves and were turned away due to weather or running out of time. The marketing (website, photos, YouTube videos) showed cave access prominently. Many felt deceived.

**Pattern:** Complaints appeared every year 2019–2024. Weather-related cave closures were normalized operationally but never properly communicated.

**Key customer statements:**
- "We paid for a cave tour and didn't even see anything" (2022)
- "I believe what really happened is we ran out of time... we spent $130 just to kayak back and forth" (2021)
- "It's a lie to call this a tour, it's a monumental rip off" — John Zambo, 8/18/22
- "My 16-year-old verbalized 'you got scammed'" (2024)
- "The tour should have been rescheduled. If it becomes too dangerous during the hour the group is paddling to the caves, those groups should be given a heavily discounted tour to try again" (2022)
- "Chloe Allen: I was under the impression we got to go 'into' the sea caves... Now, I had a great time, I just was a bit confused." (2024) — even happy customers were confused

**Fix required:** Proactive communication before entering water that caves may not be accessible that day. Consider a mandatory "caves may be unavailable" checkbox at booking, and guide script for day-of communication.

---

### 2. Tandem Kayaks Not Disclosed
**Frequency:** 15–20+ complaints across multiple years

Customers did not know they'd be in 2-person kayaks until they were on the bus or at the dock. Many would have chosen a different tour or not gone at all. Several specifically mentioned they prefer solo kayaking.

**Key customer statements:**
- "Nothing on website that we would be in tandem kayaks" — Lisa Engebretson (2020)
- "We loved it but didn't know we'd be going tandem. Would've preferred single kayaks" (2020)
- "It would have been also great to know they are sit on and not in — these were horrible for my back" — Jennifer (2019)
- "I find it much easier to use single kayaks. Second, and the biggest: I chose this tour because of the caves" — Breyann VanderVelden (2021)

**Fix required:** Website and booking confirmation must clearly state tandem kayaks. Consider offering single kayak option.

---

### 3. Guide Pacing / Group Management
**Frequency:** 20+ complaints

Guides consistently paddled too far ahead, leaving struggling customers behind. This created safety concerns (people were not visible to the guide) and ruined the experience for those who fell behind.

**Key customer statements:**
- "She took off right away and was like 200ft in front of us and the group and never checked in to see what was going on with us especially because we had 3 kids with us" — Sagina (2020)
- "Our guide didn't check back once... I was separated from my family" — Cristin Evans (2023)
- "Guide was only one person and there was many people on the trip... everyone was scattered all over the place and you couldn't hear the guide" — Kate Waits (2021)
- "This was not a guided tour. It was renting kayaks and heading out along the shoreline." — Yvonne Lutz (2021)
- "Guide commented multiple times on lack of training and staff this year." — Janet McLean (2023)

**Fix required:** Guide training standard: check on rear of group every 5–10 minutes. Two guides required for groups over 12. Never leave beginners behind.

---

### 4. Tour Description Misrepresentation (General)
**Frequency:** 20+ complaints

Beyond caves, customers felt the general experience didn't match what was advertised — cliff jumping not delivered, dunes hike not mentioned, shipwreck not visible, factual tour content lacking.

**Key customer statements:**
- "This was not a 'tour', it's an extended kayak trip up and down the shore" — multiple customers
- "The tour was nowhere close to what was promised in the online overview" — Leslie Wilson (cheese curd tour, 2023)
- "The tour guide had no information to share" — Robert Cascarano (2022)
- "It would be nice to hear more info about the caves including how they formed, type of stone, history" — Herb Ingram (2022)
- "We expected more 'touriness'... He was a great conversationalist but didn't really 'guide'" — Ben Hamilton (2023)

---

### 5. Cliff Jumping Not Delivered
**Frequency:** 10+ specific complaints

Cliff jumping is frequently shown in marketing materials and mentioned by guides/staff before tours, then cancelled mid-tour with little explanation.

**Key customer statements:**
- "We were told we would do cliff jumping and the kids were so excited... She never mentioned it again" — Sagina (2020)
- "They said we would cliff jump but we were told no when we got there. It feels to us like we were lied to" — Jennifer Valentine (2024)
- "We were rushed at the cliff jump. Would have been nice to do both sides." (2022)

---

### 6. Group Size Too Large / Safety Concerns
**Frequency:** 8–10 serious complaints, many more implied

Groups of 20+ with one guide created documented safety issues. Several customers raised specific safety concerns about beginner kayakers being left unassisted.

**Key customer statements:**
- "The group that went out was an extremely large group of kayakers; it was more than 20 people... There was only one guide... This flotilla of kayaks was spread very far out." — Heather Lutz (2021)
- "Divide large groups or at the least have two guides. Give more instruction and support to members who struggle." — Marta (2021)
- "The guide's lack of monitoring seemed irresponsible and dangerous" — Anita O'Connor (per Sharon Dossett, 2023)
- Safety incident: customer got 5 staples in head in cave (June 2022) — guide minimized the injury; "she could have bled to death as he is on blood thinners"

**Fix required:** Hard cap at 12–14 per guide. Two guides for groups 12+. Pre-tour experience screening.

---

### 7. Tour Cancellation Communication Failures
**Frequency:** 15+ complaints in 2022–2023

Customers drove 1–3 hours to find their tour cancelled with no prior notification. Multiple instances of showing up to a locked door, no phone answer.

**Key customer statements:**
- "We had to call 4 times and send 1 text before someone called us back" — Ryan Motel (2024)
- "We showed up and were told the tour was cancelled and someone should have called us" — Ann Johnston (2022)
- "We drove all the way there and when we arrived were told it was closed" — Juliana Carbo (2023)
- "We showed up half an hour early as instructed then stood there for an hour trying to contact someone" — Elly Poppe (2023)
- "You called at 7:00am to tell me the trip was changed... told 2 hours of E-bikes would be substituted. Instead I had to pay full price to rent the E-bikes." — Mary Rozendal (2020)

**Fix required:** Automated GHL workflow: weather check at 6am, cancellation text/email by 7am at latest. Confirmation phone number must be answered or have voicemail.

---

### 8. Pricing / Value Mismatch
**Frequency:** 20+ complaints

Customers felt the experience was overpriced for what was delivered, especially when caves weren't accessible or tours ran short.

**Key customer statements:**
- "$71 is insane for what we got. Honestly I wouldn't pay over $45" — Kate Waits (2021)
- "We paid over $1,400 including the obligatory tips. It was not worth that at all." — Michelle Prudlow (2022)
- "Could have rented a kayak on our own for way less... this is a monumental rip off" — John Zambo (2022)
- Suggestive tipping on receipts with minimum 18% felt coercive when experience was poor

---

### 9. Equipment Issues
**Frequency:** 10+ complaints

- Seat backs broken/sliding
- Kayaks taking on water (cracked hulls)
- Life jackets with strong body odor
- Paddle drip guards missing
- E-bike equipment: low charge, loose seats, broken pedal assist, loose handlebars

---

### 10. Beginner Instruction
**Frequency:** 10+ complaints

First-time kayakers received minimal instruction and then struggled the entire tour, unable to keep up with the group.

**Key customer statements:**
- "I've had blisters on my hands trying to keep up. One of my kids was crying thinking we would never make it back" — Brandy Anfang (2020)
- "We got almost no instructions. Get some experienced instructors please!" — Nilufar Rahman (2020)
- "Not beginner friendly... running into the dock should have been the first clue and then finally after spinning 3 times the instructor came over" (2023)
- "Justin was great... he even mentioned no one is a good paddler the first time out, so I think a little practice would have saved us some frustration" — Debbie Hines (2020)

- **Tyler/Tylor** — "amazing"
- **Drew** (2024) — "awesome and very hospitable"
- **Sean** — "great guide! He kept track of our group"

**The Setting:**
- Cave Point is consistently described as beautiful, stunning, and unique
- The water clarity, geology, and wildlife (bald eagles, pictographs) are memorable
- Cliff jumping when it happens is a highlight

**What Works Operationally (When Done Right):**
- Well-organized launch with proper safety briefing
- Photographer capturing the experience
- Drinks/snacks on half-day tours
- Dry bags and sunglasses availability

## Operational Recommendations (From Feedback Patterns)

### Immediate / Critical
1. **Cave availability communication protocol** — guide must announce at launch whether caves are accessible that day, before customers get on water. Website needs clear disclaimer.
2. **Tandem kayak disclosure** — add to website homepage, booking confirmation, and day-of email.
3. **Cancellation communication system** — 6am weather check, 7am cancellation text/email, GHL automation.
4. **Guide pacing standard** — guide must maintain visual contact with rear group member at all times.

### High Priority
5. **Group size cap** — max 12 per guide; 2 guides for groups 13+.
6. **Beginner screening** — kayaking experience question at booking; offer beginner orientation option.
7. **Equipment audit** — pre-season check of all seat backs, hull integrity, PFD cleanliness.
8. **Pricing/value alignment** — when caves are inaccessible, offer proactive partial refund or return credit.

### Medium Priority
9. **Guide narration training** — geological history, ecology, local stories. Guides should pass a content quiz.
10. **Payment system upgrade** — tap-to-pay / Apple Pay to eliminate manual CC entry complaints.
11. **Cliff jumping communication** — if cliff jumping is on the route, guide must confirm or cancel it before customers paddle out.
