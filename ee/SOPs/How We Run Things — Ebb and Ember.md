# How We Run Things
### Ebb and Ember Floating Saunas

Read this before your first session. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask Jonah to walk you through it.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

When you're hosting a session, you're running the show. You set the vibe the moment someone pulls into the parking lot. You make sure the sauna is at temp, the lounge is clean, the music is right, and the experience feels intentional.

If the propane is low, you handle it. If a guest looks confused walking up, you greet them before they have to look for you. If something breaks, you don't wait for Jonah or Davey to notice. You flag it or fix it.

This isn't a front desk job. You're the host, the guide, and the energy of the entire experience.

---

## 2. Be Real

Ebb and Ember is a wellness experience on the Willamette River, not a spa with a receptionist script. People come here to disconnect, breathe, and feel something different. The way you greet someone should feel like welcoming a friend to your spot, not checking them in.

"It's great to see you" beats "Welcome to Ebb and Ember, can I see your booking confirmation."

If someone's nervous about the cold plunge, be honest about what it feels like. If they ask about the sauna and you geek out about it, great. Authenticity is the brand.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

Show up before your guests do. Have the space ready before they arrive, not while they're watching. Follow through on what you said you'd handle.

We give grace for life stuff. But if the lounge is dirty when guests arrive, or the sauna isn't at temperature because nobody checked, that's on whoever was hosting. Own it, fix it, don't let it happen twice.

Respect also means respecting the membership system. If someone's gaming guest passes or using another member's booking, flag it. Don't look the other way because it's awkward.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If a guest gave you feedback worth sharing, post it. If you noticed the water temp display is confusing people, say something. If you figured out a better way to explain the hot-cold protocol to first-timers, share it so everyone can use it.

Write it down, send a voice memo, drop it in the channel. The format doesn't matter. What matters is it gets captured.

The best parts of this experience came from someone noticing something and speaking up. Keep doing that.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

This is a floating sauna with extreme heat, cold water, and a river. That combination requires constant attention.

Waivers signed before every session. Pregnancy disclaimers acknowledged. No one in the sauna who shouldn't be. Carbon monoxide detector working. Fire alarm working. Exit clearly marked.

If someone looks like they're overheating, you intervene. You don't wait for them to say something. If conditions on the river are sketchy, you make the call. "No jumping" means no jumping, even if the guest thinks they're being funny.

Safety equipment isn't decoration. Check it before every session.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. Bring your warmth, your curiosity, your calm. That's what makes this place special. But these five aren't optional.

If this sounds like your kind of work, you're going to love it here.
