# Social Media Strategy — Kimberlynn

*Developed from raw notes and content strategy framework. This is Kimberlynn's playbook.*

---

## The Core Idea

Don't sell the sauna. Sell the transformation.

The best-performing content in this space uses emotional storytelling, not product promotion. Every post should give someone a reason to feel something — whether that's curiosity, humor, aspiration, or calm. The brand shows up as a character, not an advertisement.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. POV Skits (TikTok-first)
Short scripted skits (15-30 seconds) that place the viewer inside a relatable moment.

**Ready-to-shoot ideas:**
- "POV: You dragged your skeptical friend to the sauna for the first time"
- "POV: It's 6am, 16 degrees outside, you're about to do your first cold plunge"
- "POV: Your coworker asks why you look so calm on a Monday"
- "POV: You used to pay $200/month for therapy. Now you have this."

Each ends with a soft close — lingering shot of the sauna, handle, or water, with just the brand handle visible. No hard sell.

**Next step:** Pick one concept. Write a 20-second script. Film it within two weeks.

---

### 2. Multi-Part Story Arc (Retention Engine)
One story told across multiple posts. Creates a reason for people to follow and come back.

**Series concepts:**
- "I used the sauna every day for 30 days" — Part 1 hook, daily check-ins, Part 30 reveal
- "We brought a different person every week" — athlete, skeptic, anxious person, elderly parent, first-timer
- "The science behind why you feel amazing after a sauna" — broken into bite-size episodes

End every video with: "Part 2 drops tomorrow — follow so you don't miss it."

**Next step:** Choose one series and map out the arc (how many parts, what each covers). The 30-day challenge is the easiest to start — it requires only committing to the format.

---

### 3. Niche Humor and Relatable Content
Hyper-specific content that makes sauna regulars feel seen and pulls curious people in.

**Ideas:**
- "Sauna people vs. gym people" — comparison skit
- "The 5 types of people in the sauna" — the over-sweater, the zen master, the first-timer, the bro, the talker
- "Things only sauna people understand" — listicle Reel format
- "Me explaining to my family why I need a $3,000 sauna" — relatable overspend humor

**Next step:** Identify which of these fits Kimberlynn's natural on-camera style. Start with the format she's most comfortable delivering.

---

### 4. Edu-tainment (Fast Value Hooks)
Short, sharp, informative content. Works well as faceless B-roll with text overlay.

**Ideas:**
- "3 things that happen to your body in the first 10 minutes of a sauna"
- "Why Finnish people live longer" — cultural storytelling
- "The hot-cold protocol athletes use for recovery"
- "Sauna myths, debunked" — hot take format

**Next step:** Write 3 of these as scripts and film them in one session as faceless B-roll. Lower barrier than on-camera content, good for building the content bank.

---

### 5. Authentic Transformation Stories
Real, unpolished moments that document the emotional arc of a visit.

**Ideas:**
- Before and after mood — film yourself arriving tired and stressed, leaving calm
- "My morning routine" with the sauna as the anchor
- Day-in-the-life that includes a sauna session
- Raw unscripted guest reactions

**Next step:** Film the before/after at least once. This is easy to capture organically on a real session day.

---

## Hook Formula (First 3 Seconds)

Every video needs a pattern interrupt in the opening frame. Use one of these openers:

- **Curiosity gap:** "I didn't believe this until I tried it for 30 days..."
- **Bold claim:** "This is the cheapest therapy you'll find."
- **Conflict:** "My doctor told me not to. I did it anyway."
- **Relatability:** "You know that feeling when stress just leaves your body?"
- **Visual shock:** Open mid-cold-plunge or in rising steam, then cut to the story.

---

## Video Structure

1. **Hook** (0-3 sec) — emotional or visual disruption
2. **Promise** (3-8 sec) — tell them what they'll get by watching
3. **Deliver** (8-50 sec) — the skit, story, or value
4. **Close** (last 3 sec) — soft CTA: "Part 2 tomorrow" or "Would you try this?"

---

## Posting Schedule

| Platform | Content Type | Target Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | POV skits, niche humor, trending audio | 5-7x/week |
| Instagram Reels | Aesthetic B-roll, edu-tainment, story series | 3-4x/week |
| Instagram Stories | Behind-the-scenes, polls, Q&A | Daily |
| YouTube Shorts | Repurposed best performers | 2-3x/week |
| YouTube Long-form | 30-day challenge docs, build series | 1x/week |

Short-form attracts. Long-form builds loyalty. Short-form is the trailer. Long-form is the film.

---

## Immediate Action Plan for Kimberlynn

1. **Choose one content pillar to start.** POV skits or niche humor are the lowest barrier. Pick one concept from those lists.
2. **Write one script this week.** Keep it under 30 seconds. Practice the hook out loud.
3. **Film one piece of content this week.** It doesn't have to be perfect. Post it.
4. **Set up a content bank folder.** Start saving B-roll, reactions, steam shots, water shots, and ambient footage every time you're on the boat. This is the raw material for faceless content.
5. **Pick a posting cadence you can actually hold.** It is better to post 3x/week consistently than 7x/week for one week and then go quiet.

## Notes

- The goal is not one viral moment. The goal is a community that comes back.
- Kimberlynn should own this channel and its voice. The content should feel like her, not like a brand account.
- Keep a running list of hooks and ideas. Anything that makes you laugh or stop scrolling personally is worth adapting.
