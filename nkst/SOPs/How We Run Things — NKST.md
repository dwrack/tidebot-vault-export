# How We Run Things
### New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours

Read this before your first day. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask your manager to walk you through it or drop into the Slack channel.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

The bayou is yours. You know the water, you know the wildlife, you know when the light hits the cypress trees just right. Nobody in Portland or Wisconsin is going to tell you how to run a tour in Manchac.

If a local restaurant wants to bring their staff out, make the connection. If the launch site needs something, handle it. If a guest asks where to eat, you don't shrug, you send them somewhere you'd actually go.

You're not filling a seat in the van. You're running a piece of this business every time you paddle out.

---

## 2. Be Real

We are not another generic swamp tour. We don't bait gators with marshmallows. We don't blast through the ecosystem. We teach people about what's actually happening out there, and we do it because we give a shit.

That means you talk from what you know. Your knowledge of the swamp, the ecology, the history. If a guest asks something you don't know, say so. Real beats rehearsed every time.

Same with each other. If something's not working, say it directly. Don't hide behind positive language. This crew has been together long enough to be honest.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

This is the line. Show up ready. Follow through. Don't let things slide because the conversation is awkward.

We give grace. Everyone has off days. But there's a difference between a rough week and checking out. If you've lost the energy for this, have the conversation. We'd rather help you figure it out than watch you coast.

Respect also means: don't take tours that should go to someone who needs the hours. Don't leave the van trashed. Don't skip the post-tour checklist because you're in a hurry. The person after you is counting on you.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

You've been doing this for years. You know things that new guides need to learn. Share it.

Captain's log at the end of a tour. A voice memo on the drive back. A photo dropped in the channel. Doesn't matter the format. What matters is it gets captured somewhere the team can find it.

If you figured out a better route for high water, share it. If a guest asked a question that stumped you, post it so we can all get smarter. The best SOPs come from the people actually doing the work.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

Waivers get signed on every tour. No exceptions. Gear gets checked. Life jackets get inspected. If someone shows up too impaired to paddle, they don't go out.

A guide who skips waiver checks isn't just cutting a corner. They're saying the safety of our guests doesn't matter enough to spend 2 minutes on. That's a termination conversation.

We've seen what happens when safety gets treated like a suggestion across our other businesses. We won't make that mistake here. If you see something unsafe, say it immediately.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. You can be great at this job in a hundred different ways, and this crew already is. But these five aren't optional.

If this sounds like your kind of operation, you're in the right place.
