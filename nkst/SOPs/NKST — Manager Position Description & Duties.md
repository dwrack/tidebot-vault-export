# Operations Manager — New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours

**Company:** Apres Assets LLC dba New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours / Nola Kayak Tours
**Reports To:** David (Jeffrey), Owner
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

> **Legal Note — Contractor Classification**
> This position may be structured as a 1099 independent contractor engagement. Louisiana applies a three-part test under La. R.S. 23:1472(12)(E): (A) freedom from control over the manner and means of work, (B) work performed outside the usual course of the company's business, and (C) independently established trade or occupation. The manager role carries inherent classification risk because managing core operations is closely tied to the company's usual course of business. This document is written to maximize contractor defensibility, but it does not eliminate that risk. Have a Louisiana-licensed employment attorney review the classification decision and any contractor agreement before engagement begins. The ICA template in the vault Legal folder applies to guides — a separate agreement should be drafted for the manager role.

## The Short Version

You're the face of one of the most unique outdoor experiences in New Orleans. Your job is to be out in the community building relationships, showing up at events, and converting group bookings into revenue. The administrative overhead — scheduling, guest communications, compliance tracking, payroll prep, review monitoring — is handled by a combination of GoHighLevel automation and Claude AI. You oversee those systems, act on exceptions, and spend the majority of your time doing the work that actually moves the needle: community presence, partnership development, and guide leadership.

This role used to be heavy. We've rebuilt the systems around it so it doesn't have to be.

## Employment Classification

This engagement may be structured as either:

**W-2 Employee** — Standard employment with payroll taxes withheld, set hours and oversight, and access to company benefits as applicable. Recommended if the day-to-day work is substantially directed by the owner or if the manager works exclusively for NKST.

**1099 Independent Contractor** — The contractor determines the methods, sequence, and manner of achieving the agreed outcomes. Compensation is a flat monthly retainer for defined scope, paid bi-monthly via PayPal. The contractor is responsible for their own tax obligations. A signed Independent Contractor Agreement (separate from the guide ICA) is required before work begins.

**If structured as 1099:** The contractor must be free to determine how they achieve results. They may work for other clients. The company specifies outcomes — not hours, not methods, not daily activity counts. The language in this document reflects that structure. Any day-to-day direction from the owner should be framed as outcome clarification, not task assignment.

Classification should be determined in consultation with Apres Assets LLC's attorney or accountant before the engagement begins.

## Scope of Engagement

The Operations Manager is engaged to produce the following outcomes for New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours on an ongoing basis. The contractor determines the methods, schedule, and approach used to achieve these outcomes, subject to the quality standards and result requirements described below.

---

## Core Outcomes

### Community Presence and Partnership Development

The primary value of this role is physical presence in the New Orleans tourism and hospitality community — the relationships built, the referrals generated, and the events organized. No automation replaces this.

The contractor is responsible for:

- Developing and maintaining referral relationships with hotels, concierges, Airbnb hosts, event coordinators, and local hospitality professionals
- Logging partner contacts in the company CRM — automated follow-up sequences run from there
- Representing NKST at relevant local events, tourism mixers, environmental programs, and community initiatives
- Developing corporate partnership opportunities: team-building groups, employee experiences, buyout packages
- Coordinating and supporting special events: seasonal launches, themed tours, volunteer cleanups, and community programs
- Inviting hospitality professionals and travel writers to experience the tour firsthand
- Growing and maintaining the affiliate and referral network as a sustained, measurable outcome over the term of the engagement

Target outcome: measurable growth in referral-sourced bookings and active partner relationships over each 90-day period, reviewed with the owner quarterly.

### Group Booking Conversion

Automation captures group leads and sends an initial response. The contractor's job is to convert them. Group bookings (bachelorette parties, corporate teams, private events) represent the highest revenue per booking and require a human touch to close.

The contractor is responsible for:

- Following up on group leads within a reasonable window of inquiry capture
- Qualifying the group: size, date, occasion, shuttle needs, custom requests
- Providing quotes based on current pricing; escalating custom or private tour pricing to the owner
- Booking confirmed groups in FareHarbor
- Managing the group relationship through to tour day

### Guide Team Leadership

Scheduling logistics — manifests, confirmations, compliance reminders, payroll tallies — are handled by automation. The contractor's role with guides is the part automation cannot do: keeping skilled guides engaged, growing their craft, and making them want to stay.

The contractor is responsible for:

- Maintaining the guide relationship through periodic check-ins and coaching conversations
- Using weekly guide field notes (synthesized by Claude) to identify coaching themes and act on them
- Supporting guide skill development: storytelling, wildlife knowledge, guest handling, safety practices
- Managing guide no-shows, sick calls, and last-minute swaps when they occur
- Onboarding new guides through the compliance checklist before their first tour; confirming all required documents are complete before scheduling
- Escalating guide performance or conduct issues to the owner with documentation

### Operations Oversight

The automation handles routine guest and guide touchpoints. The contractor's job is to verify the systems are working and intervene when they're not.

The contractor is responsible for:

- Reviewing a daily briefing (prepared by Claude) covering: current tours, guide confirmation status, weather, and any flagged issues
- Checking the GHL conversations inbox for bot escalations requiring a human response
- Auditing FareHarbor periodically for data accuracy: guide assignments, third-party shuttle flags, extended tour participant counts
- Making weather go/no-go decisions on any day tours are scheduled, and communicating those decisions to guests and guides before 7:00 AM on tour day -- this decision is always the contractor's to make
- Checking the Honey Island water gauge before any Honey Island tour runs
- Identifying workflow failures and correcting the underlying data issue

### Fleet and Equipment Accountability

The contractor is responsible for:

- Overseeing vehicle readiness: fuel, cleanliness, and mechanical status at the company's staging location (1407 Piety St)
- Reviewing the weekly vehicle accountability log (compiled from guide post-tour check-ins and Claude's summary)
- Coordinating repair, maintenance, or supply needs and escalating capital expenditures to the owner
- Ensuring equipment inventory is maintained: PFDs, paddles, safety kit, merchandise

### Compliance and Permit Oversight

The contractor is responsible for:

- Monitoring guide compliance status: CPR certifications, Louisiana guiding permits, signed ICAs, and driver's license records
- Ensuring no guide is scheduled for a tour unless all compliance items are current
- Preparing and submitting quarterly LDWF Eco-Tour Special Use Permit reports (January, April, July, October) using the formatted export Claude prepares from FareHarbor data
- Filing incident reports for any on-tour injury or emergency; notifying the owner immediately

### Marketing Support

The contractor supports the company's marketing presence as part of the engagement scope. The contractor determines the methods and timing for achieving these outcomes within the period of the engagement.

The contractor is responsible for:

- Supporting social media presence: contributing content, captions, and posts on an ongoing basis
- Ensuring guides post required tour photos to Google after each tour
- Responding to online reviews (Google, TripAdvisor, Viator) in a timely manner -- Claude drafts responses for review
- Supporting seasonal campaigns and promotions as directed by the owner

## What Claude Handles

Claude operates as a background operations resource. These are not tasks the contractor performs manually -- they are outputs the contractor reviews, approves, and acts on.

**Daily:**
- Morning briefing: tour summary, guide status, weather check, open flags
- Draft responses to escalated guest inquiries
- Draft review responses for contractor review and posting
- Social media captions from guide photos and field notes

**Weekly:**
- Ops summary: tours run, guide activity, compliance flags, automation health
- Payroll calculation from FareHarbor export
- Vehicle accountability log summary
- Chatbot accuracy review: recurring escalations flagged for FAQ update
- Content additions from the week's field notes and tour activity

**Guide-facing:**
- Compliance reminders sent automatically
- Post-tour check-in synthesis and coaching theme summary
- Tour knowledge resource for guides between training sessions

**Quarterly:**
- LDWF report formatted from FareHarbor CSV for contractor review and submission

## What Guides Own

A small set of consistent post-tour responsibilities keeps guides connected to the operation without adding meaningful burden.

After every tour:
- Reply to the GHL post-tour check-in text (three yes/no questions about van, gas, and PFDs)
- Post one photo to Google (required -- tied to pay)

Ongoing:
- Notify management in advance of any unavailability
- Upload compliance documents when prompted (CPR renewal, updated license)
- Flag equipment issues or van concerns immediately after a tour

## Contractor Independence

If this engagement is structured as 1099, the following applies:

- The contractor determines their own schedule, hours, and methods for achieving the outcomes described above
- The contractor is free to perform operations management, consulting, or other work for other clients, provided it does not create a conflict of interest with NKST
- The contractor provides advance notice of extended unavailability as a professional courtesy -- not as a request for approval
- The company will not withhold income taxes, Social Security, or Medicare from payments; the contractor is responsible for all self-employment tax obligations
- The contractor uses their own judgment in determining how to achieve the outcomes above; the company specifies what results are needed, not how to produce them

## What This Role Is Not Responsible For

The following decisions belong to the owner. The contractor surfaces relevant information and makes a recommendation; the owner decides.

- Pricing changes to any tour product
- Hiring or terminating guides (contractor may recommend; owner decides)
- Changes to the legal structure of Apres Assets LLC
- Signing contracts with vendors, platforms, or partners
- Responding to legal threats, chargebacks, or insurance claims
- Capital expenditures
- Changes to the FareHarbor product catalog
- Public statements on behalf of the company
- Custom pricing for private tour buyouts
- Any matter involving the LDWF permit beyond routine quarterly reporting
