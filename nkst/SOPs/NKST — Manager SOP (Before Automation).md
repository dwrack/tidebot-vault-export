## Booking Management

### New Bookings (FareHarbor)
1. FareHarbor sends an automatic confirmation email to the guest when they book. You do not need to resend that confirmation — it goes out automatically.
2. Review new bookings at least twice daily: morning opening routine + mid-afternoon check.
3. For each new booking, verify:
   - [ ] Tour type is correct
   - [ ] Shuttle option matches what was purchased (third-party = always shuttle)
   - [ ] Guest count is within capacity
   - [ ] A guide is assigned or needs to be assigned
4. Assign a guide if one is not yet assigned:
   - Check guide availability (you maintain this mentally or in a shared note/calendar — there is no automated tool for this yet)
   - Text the guide: *"Hey [name], can you take [tour name] on [date] at [time]? [X] guests. Let me know ASAP."*
   - Do not list a guide as confirmed in FareHarbor until they have replied yes

### Cancellations & Reschedules
1. When a guest cancels via FareHarbor, the platform processes the refund per your cancellation policy settings. Review to make sure it processed correctly.
2. When a guest calls or texts to cancel:
   - Pull their booking in FareHarbor
   - Process cancellation or reschedule manually in FareHarbor
   - Confirm with the guest by text or email: *"Your booking has been [cancelled/rescheduled]. [Refund or new date details]."*
3. Notify the assigned guide of any cancellation that affects their tour: *"FYI — the [time] tour on [date] was cancelled. You're off."*

### Third-Party Bookings (Viator, Expedia, TripAdvisor)
1. These bookings always include a shuttle pickup at 740 N. Rampart St.
2. When you pull the manifest for a day with third-party bookings, manually verify the shuttle is listed for each third-party guest.
3. If the shuttle is missing from the manifest, update it in FareHarbor and re-alert the guide.

## Customer Communication

**Priority order for incoming inquiries: phone first, then text, then email.**

### Incoming Phone Calls
1. Answer every call during business hours if at all possible.
2. If you miss a call: call back within 30 minutes. Do not let missed calls sit.
3. Common calls and how to handle them:
   - **"I want to book a tour"** — Direct them to the website, or take their info and book manually in FareHarbor if they prefer. Get: name, email, phone, tour type, date, number of guests, shuttle needed.
   - **"I have a booking question"** — Pull their booking in FareHarbor. Answer from there.
   - **"I need to cancel/reschedule"** — See Cancellations & Reschedules above.
   - **"What should I bring?"** — Comfortable clothes you can get wet, closed-toe shoes or water shoes, sunscreen, insect repellent, water bottle. No valuables. Life jackets provided.
   - **"Is there a weight limit?"** — Our tandem kayaks hold up to 400 lbs total for two paddlers. Solo paddlers most comfortable under 250 lbs. Tell them this politely.
   - **"What if it rains?"** — Tours run in light rain. We cancel for lightning, severe weather, or unsafe water levels. If we cancel, guests receive a full refund or reschedule option.
   - **"I didn't get a confirmation"** — Check spam first. If they still don't have it, pull up their booking in FareHarbor and verify it exists, then resend the confirmation from FareHarbor.
   - **"I forgot to add transportation"** — Verify space is available on the van for their tour, then add the shuttle manually in FareHarbor. Collect payment if needed. Confirm the new total and pickup location (740 N. Rampart St.) with the guest.

### Incoming Texts (Company Number)
1. Check texts every 2 hours during business hours.
2. Respond within 2 hours. Keep responses concise and friendly.
3. If a text is complex (complaint, group inquiry, refund request), call the person back instead of trying to resolve it over text.

### Incoming Email
1. Check email morning and afternoon.
2. Respond to all emails within 4 hours during business hours.
3. Group inquiry emails: see Group Inquiries section below.
4. Review inquiry emails (from Viator, Google, etc.): respond with direct booking link if they're not already booked.

### Group & Private Event Inquiries
1. When a group inquiry comes in (phone, text, or email), gather the following before quoting:
   - Group size
   - Preferred date(s) and time
   - Tour type preference
   - Private or public tour preference
   - Shuttle needed?
   - Any special occasions (birthday, bachelorette, corporate)?
2. Quote based on current pricing:
   - Manchac 2-Hour: $65 (+ $25 shuttle if needed)
   - Extended Manchac 4-Hour: $130 (+ $25 shuttle)
   - Whitney + Swamp Combo: $195 (shuttle included)
   - Honey Island 2-Hour: $65 (+ $25 shuttle) — **summer only**
3. If the group wants a private tour, escalate to owner for custom pricing discussion.
4. Follow up on every group inquiry within 24 hours if you don't hear back after initial quote.

## Tour Switch Protocol

Tours are sometimes moved to a different location (e.g., Manchac ↔ Honey Island) due to participation, water levels, or other operational factors. This protocol ensures guests are notified and the switch is documented.

### When a Switch is Authorized
- Honey Island gauge is out of range (move to Manchac) — see Weather Decision Protocol
- Participation consolidation (owner or manager authorized)
- Guide or resource availability issue

### Notification Steps
1. Identify all affected guests from the FareHarbor manifest.
2. Text each guest using the tour switch template below. Do this before tour day whenever possible — same-day switches cause the most guest anxiety.
3. Update FareHarbor booking notes with: original location, new location, reason for switch, date/time of guest notification.
4. Confirm the switch with the assigned guide by text.

**Tour Switch Outbound Template:**
> Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] from NolaKayak Tours. Quick update on your [TIME] tour [DATE]: we'll be heading to [NEW LOCATION] instead of [ORIGINAL LOCATION] due to [brief reason — e.g., "scheduling" or "water levels"]. Same great experience, same guides, same pickup! Your pickup at [PICKUP LOCATION] at [PICKUP TIME] remains unchanged. Any questions? Text or call 504-571-9975.

## Weather Decision Protocol

**Weather cancellations must be communicated to all guests no later than 7:00 AM on the day of the tour.**

### Decision Authority
- The manager makes the go/no-go call on weather independently using the criteria below.
- If the manager is uncertain, they call the owner. The owner has final say.

### Cancellation Criteria
Cancel or reroute if any of the following apply:
- Active lightning warning or severe thunderstorm warning in the tour area
- Sustained winds over 25 mph forecast during tour window
- Honey Island gauge below 5 ft or above 12 ft (reroute to Manchac — do not cancel unless Manchac is also unsafe)
- Other unsafe water or weather condition at manager's discretion

### How to Notify Guests (Manual Process)
1. Pull the manifest from FareHarbor for all affected tours.
2. Call every guest on the manifest. This is the priority — do it first.
3. For guests who don't answer: leave a voicemail AND send a text:
   *"Hi, this is [name] from New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours. Due to [weather/water conditions], today's [tour name] at [time] has been cancelled/rerouted. We're happy to reschedule you or provide a full refund. Please call or text us at 504-571-9975."*
4. Send the same message via email to every guest on the manifest.
5. Notify the assigned guide(s) by text immediately after reaching out to guests.
6. Process refunds or reschedules in FareHarbor for any guests who request them.
7. Document the cancellation: date, tour(s) affected, reason, number of guests notified, time notifications went out.

## Hurricane / Named Storm Protocol

This protocol is distinct from the regular weather cancellation process. A named storm (hurricane or tropical storm) requires proactive action across all upcoming bookings, not just same-day tours.

**Monitor:** National Hurricane Center (nhc.noaa.gov) during hurricane season (June 1 – November 30).

### Activation Threshold
Activate this protocol when a named storm watch or warning is issued within 200 miles of the New Orleans metro area, OR when the NHC forecast cone includes the region within 72 hours.

### Steps
1. **Notify owner immediately.** Do not proceed without owner awareness.
2. **Cancel all tours proactively** for the affected period (minimum: next 72 hours, potentially longer based on storm track).
3. **Batch text all upcoming guests** for the affected dates using this template:
   > Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] from NolaKayak Tours. Due to [STORM NAME], we are proactively cancelling all tours [DATE RANGE] for the safety of our guests and guides. You will receive a full refund automatically — no action needed on your part. We'll be in touch to reschedule when we reopen. Please stay safe!
4. **Process full refunds** in FareHarbor for all affected bookings. Do not wait for guests to request refunds.
5. **Update Google Business Profile** hours/status to reflect closure.
6. **Post on social media** that tours are suspended due to the named storm.
7. **Block out dates** in FareHarbor availability to prevent new bookings during the closure period.
8. **Document:** dates cancelled, number of guests notified, time notifications went out, total refunds processed.
9. **Reactivation:** Do not reopen for tours until the owner approves restart and the launch site has been inspected post-storm.

## Emergency Escalation Protocol

### On-Water Emergency (Guest Injury, Capsize, Medical Event)
1. Guide calls 911 immediately for any life-threatening situation
2. Guide calls manager
3. Manager calls owner
4. Guide stays with guests until emergency services arrive
5. Manager documents: date, time, tour, guide name, what happened, who was injured, what action was taken
6. Collect witness statements from other guests as soon as reasonably possible
7. Do not discuss liability, fault, or insurance with guests or media — direct all such questions to owner
8. File written incident report within 24 hours

### Van Breakdown
1. Guide calls manager immediately
2. Manager arranges alternative transportation if guests are stranded
3. Manager calls owner
4. Manager arranges tow or roadside assistance
5. Document incident

### Guide No-Show (see Guide No-Show Protocol above)

### Guest Complaint / Threat of Legal Action
1. Listen to the complaint fully — do not argue
2. Apologize that the experience didn't meet expectations
3. Offer to make it right within your authority: partial refund, reschedule, etc.
4. If the guest mentions an attorney, a lawsuit, or formal complaint: stop, thank them for their feedback, and tell them the owner will follow up directly
5. Call owner immediately after the call
