# Guide Communication System — SMS + Email
*Created: 2026-03-30 | Build in GHL with Chevy*

> **Core philosophy:** Guides who feel seen, educated, and connected to something bigger don't leave. This system isn't just about information delivery — it's about culture. Every message should make them feel like a valued part of something real, not like a contractor getting a memo.

---

## SMS vs Email — The Decision

**Use SMS for:** Everything personal, motivational, time-sensitive, and cultural.
**Use Email for:** Documents, handbooks, long-form content, things they need to reference.

| | SMS | Email |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | ~98% within 3 min | ~20–30% |
| Where guides read it | In the van, on the dock, between tours | At home, maybe |
| Tone | Teammate | Company |
| Best for | Recognition, facts, culture, check-ins | W-9, ICA, handbooks, monthly deep dives |
| Cost in GHL | ~$0.01–0.02/message (~$2/month for 13 guides) | Included in subscription |

**Verdict: SMS-primary. Email-monthly. Automate both.**

---

## The Weekly SMS Rotation

*Runs on a 4-week cycle during active season (March–October). Each week has a theme so guides get variety — they're never getting the same kind of message twice in a row.*

---

### Week 1 — Swamp School 🌿
*Give them something they can use on tours that week.*

The best guides know things. This makes them better at their job in real time — and it shows you're investing in their craft, not just their schedule.

**Format:** 1–3 sentences. A fact, a story hook, or a talking point.

**Examples:**
> "Swamp School: The cypress knees you see poking out of the water aren't for breathing — scientists think they help anchor the tree in soft wetland soil. Great thing to point out when guests ask. Have a good one out there 🌊"

> "Swamp School: Louisiana loses a football field of wetland every hour to erosion. One powerful line to drop when guests ask why we do eco tours. See you out there."

> "Swamp School: Roseate spoonbills weren't spotted in Louisiana for decades — they're back because of conservation efforts. If you see one, point it out. Guests never forget it."

> "Swamp School: The word 'bayou' is Choctaw — so is 'manchac.' This land had names and people long before the city did. Good context when guests ask about the history."

*Build a library of 20–30 of these. Rotate seasonally. Update as new things happen (restoration milestones, wildlife sightings, climate news).*

---

### Week 2 — Recognition 🙌
*Call someone out. Celebrate something specific.*

Most contractors never hear "you did great" unless something went wrong. This week is the antidote. Name names. Be specific. It costs nothing and it means everything.

**Format:** 1–4 sentences. Name a guide, a review, a moment, or a guest interaction.

**Examples:**
> "Shoutout to River this week — guest left a 5-star review and mentioned her by name. Said she made them feel totally safe and kept pointing out wildlife they would've paddled past. That's the job done right."

> "Josh asked a guest post-tour if they needed any help getting an Uber — turns out they did. Small thing, huge deal to them. That's the extra 5% that turns a good tour into a story they tell for years."

> "This week's review: 'Our guide knew the name of every bird and made the whole swamp feel alive.' That's what we're out here doing. Keep it up."

> "First week of the season and the group handled it well. No drama, good weather, kayaks came back clean. That's a win. Thank you all."

*Pull these from: Google reviews, FareHarbor feedback, personal observations, anything you notice. Keep a running list of moments to celebrate.*

---

### Week 3 — Vision Share 🌎
*Connect their daily work to something bigger.*

Guides show up for a tour. The best ones show up for a mission. This week reminds them what that mission is — without being preachy. Share something real: a milestone, a partnership, a grant, a restoration update, a plan.

**Format:** 2–4 sentences. Share a piece of the bigger picture.

**Examples:**
> "Quick update: we were selected for a Climate Culture grant this year. Means we're building sustainable infrastructure at the marina — water catchment, solar, art installations. Your tours literally fund this. Appreciate you."

> "Sankofa Second Saturday is back this month. We bring kayaks, they bring community. Every person you put on the water in the Lower 9th is someone who might become a conservationist. Big picture stuff."

> "We had 10,000 visitors paddle through last year. Every one of them left with a different idea of what a swamp is. That's yours. That's what you did."

> "Common Ground Relief planted 300 new cypress trees in Bayou Sauvage this spring. You're guiding tours in a forest that's growing back. That doesn't happen everywhere."

---

### Week 4 — Their Voice ❓
*Ask them something. Actually use the answer.*

The fastest way to make someone feel invisible is to never ask what they think. The fastest way to make them feel valued is to ask — and then follow through. This week creates a feedback loop that makes the company better AND makes guides feel like partners.

**Format:** One genuine question. Keep it easy to answer by text.

**Examples:**
> "Quick question: What's the most common question guests ask that you don't know the answer to? Want to make sure we get it into the handbook."

> "What's the weirdest or coolest thing you've seen on the water this month? Asking for the newsletter 👀"

> "If you could change one thing about how we do pre-tour logistics, what would it be? Genuinely asking."

> "What's one thing you wish you'd known before your first solo tour?"

> "Scale of 1–10: how comfortable do you feel handling a capsized kayak situation? Asking so we know who needs extra practice before the season peaks."

*Aggregate answers. Put the good ones in the handbook. Share anonymous versions back to the group. Let guides see their input actually changed something.*

## The Monthly Email — "The Swamp Letter"

*Send on the 1st of each month during season. Longer form, more personal, goes somewhere guides actually sit and read.*

**Content structure (keep it under 500 words):**

1. **Opening line:** Something from the bayou — a sighting, a moment, a season update. Not "Hi team."
2. **Feature:** One deeper story. Ecology, history, a guest moment, a conservation win, a guide spotlight.
3. **What's coming:** Next month's schedule notes, events, anything to put on their radar.
4. **One ask or resource:** A link to something useful (new talking point, training resource, review link), or a question they can reply to.
5. **Close:** Short. Human. Not corporate.

**Example opening lines that set the right tone:**
> "The spoonbills are back."
> "Had a guest cry on the water last Saturday. In the best way."
> "Manchac is different in April — quieter, greener, waking up."
> "We hit 10,000 paddle-outs last season. Here's what that means."

**Subject line formula:** Something from the swamp, not a corporate subject line.
- ✅ "The spoonbills are back + April schedule notes"
- ✅ "What a guest said about you last week"
- ❌ "Monthly Guide Update — April 2026"
- ❌ "Important: Please Read"

## Automated Operational Texts (Always-On)

These run year-round, triggered by events — not a calendar.

| Trigger | Message | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Guide scheduled for tour | "Hey [Name] — you're on for [Tour] on [Date] at [Time]. Guests will be at [Location]. Reply if anything comes up." | 48 hrs before |
| Tour starts in 2 hours | "You're up in 2 hours. Van check, waiver confirm, weather looks [X]. Text if you need anything." | 2 hrs before |
| Tour end time passes | "How'd the tour go? Anything to flag — guest issue, equipment, wildlife moment? Quick reply is fine." | 1 hr after |
| Guide hasn't responded in 24hrs (post-tour) | Flag to Michelle / management | 24 hrs after tour |
| Guide birthday (if collected) | "Happy birthday [Name] 🎂 Appreciate you being part of the crew." | Day of |

## Why This Works

This isn't a communication system — it's a leadership system delivered through a phone.

Guides who receive Week 1 become **better at their job.**
Guides who receive Week 2 feel **seen and valued.**
Guides who receive Week 3 feel **part of something.**
Guides who receive Week 4 feel **like partners, not contractors.**

Repeat that cycle for a full season and you have guides who come back, recommend the job to friends, give tours that get 5-star reviews, and represent the brand the way you want it represented.

The cost: ~$2/month in SMS fees and 2 hours per month to write fresh content.
The alternative: high turnover, inconsistent guest experience, and Michelle manually texting 13 people.
