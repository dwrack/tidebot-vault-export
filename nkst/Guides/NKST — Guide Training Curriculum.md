## Overview

This curriculum takes a new guide from zero to certified solo in four phases. Each phase has a clear sign-off requirement before advancing. No one leads a tour solo without completing all four phases. This is not about liability paperwork — it's about making sure guests have a genuinely great experience and come home safe.

Estimated time to certification: 2–4 weeks depending on tour availability and your learning pace.

## Phase 1: Self-Study

**Goal:** Arrive at your first shadow shift able to answer basic guest questions and understand the full day-of procedure without prompting.

**What to memorize:**

- All tour names, prices, and shuttle add-on costs
- Tour times: 9am, 11:30am, 2pm, 4:30pm
- Kayak weight limit (400 lbs per kayak), age minimum (8+), tandem rule for under-16
- PFD rules: required 100% on water; USCG Type III
- The 10 common customer questions and their answers (see Tour Script doc)
- All paddle signals: vertical static = come to me; horizontal = stop; 45° = direction; vertical waving = distress
- Emergency procedures for lightning, capsize, medical, and lost guest
- Guide conduct rules (no substances, no profanity, phone away while driving, uniform on)
- Gas station procedure: Shell only, card code 6006
- Van base address: 1407 Piety St | Pickup: 740 N. Rampart St (Rampart Treehouse)
- Key contact: Manager Michelle Healey (216) 262-3865

**Ecology to know cold:**

- What cypress knees are (root stabilizers — not for breathing)
- Why the water is brown (tannins from decomposing cypress — tea analogy)
- What water hyacinth is and why it matters (invasive; blocks light; recedes in winter = deeper access)
- Louisiana wetland loss statistic: 40% of US coastal wetlands; a football field per hour
- Manchac logging history and current trees as regrowth (conservation arc)
- Alligator size estimation: snout-to-eye distance in inches = approximate body length in feet
- Alligator safety: shy around humans; no recorded kayak attack here; we never bait

**Phase 1 Sign-Off:** Pass the Knowledge Checkpoint below (verbal Q&A with manager or senior guide).

## Phase 2: Shadow Shifts

**Minimum:** 2 full shadow shifts on different tour types (e.g., one standard Manchac, one with shuttle guests).

**What to observe:**

- How the lead guide texts guests the night before and what they say
- Van pick-up and pre-check at Piety St (tires, fuel, PFDs, paddles, cleanliness)
- Pickup timing and positioning at 740 N. Rampart
- FareHarbor check-in flow; how 3rd-party bookings are handled (always get shuttle)
- Gas station stop: how guide manages the group (restroom, water, timing)
- Trailer backing at the launch site; why customers do not park in the motorized boat area
- Waiver QR code check (on back of van seat)
- Safety briefing — listen for the exact language used; note tone and pacing
- How content stops are chosen and paced (2–3 stops, 5–10 min each)
- How the guide handles questions they don't know the answer to
- Equipment collection at the end; how the review/tip ask is made
- Post-tour van reset: full tank, clean van, hang PFDs, lock Piety St

**What to practice on Shadow Shift 2:**

- Deliver the safety briefing to the lead guide (do it before guests arrive, or debrief after)
- Attempt the paddle signals demo
- Call out at least one wildlife or ecology talking point on the water (lead guide cues you in)

**Shadow Shift Sign-Off Checklist:**

- [ ] Observed full day-of procedure start to finish
- [ ] Can recite all paddle signals without prompting
- [ ] Delivered practice safety briefing (lead guide sign-off)
- [ ] Demonstrated PFD fitting on a guest or mannequin
- [ ] Backed trailer at least once under supervision
- [ ] Demonstrated FareHarbor check-in on a real or test manifest
- [ ] Knows waiver QR location and process
- [ ] Reviewed post-tour reset procedure

## Phase 3: Supervised Solo

**What this is:** You lead the tour from start to finish. The manager or a senior guide is present as a silent observer — they do not intervene unless safety is at risk. After the tour, you debrief together.

**Pre-tour prep:**

- Text guests yourself the night before (manager reviews draft first)
- Pick up van and complete the full pre-check independently
- Arrive at 740 N. Rampart 5–10 min before guests

**Debrief structure (post-tour):**

1. Self-assessment first: What went well? What felt rough?
2. Observer feedback: Specific notes on guest engagement, safety briefing accuracy, paddle signals, content stops
3. Emergency scenario Q&A: Observer picks one scenario; you walk through your response
4. Review ask debrief: Did you ask? How did it land?
5. Go / No-go for Phase 4

**Common Phase 3 gaps to watch for:**

- Rushing the safety briefing (slow down — this is non-negotiable)
- Forgetting headcounts at stops
- Under-using paddle signals on the water
- Not asking for the review at the end
- Forgetting to fill the gas tank before returning the van

**Phase 3 Sign-Off:** Observer completes written debrief form and signs off. Manager reviews and approves advancement.

## Phase 4: Certified Solo

**Congratulations.** You're cleared to lead tours independently.

**Ongoing standards:**

- Uniform (kayak shirt) on for every tour, no exceptions
- LDWF permit card on your person every tour day
- No personal phone use while driving the van
- No substances before or during tours; no profanity or inappropriate conduct around guests
- Post-tour reset completed before leaving (gas, clean van, PFDs hung, Piety St locked)
- Social or Google post after tours encouraged (manager will brief on brand tone)
- Conduct violations: 1st = full equipment scrub + re-read this manual; 2nd = management review; 3rd = termination

**Annual renewal:**

- LDWF guide permit cards expire December 31. Renew before then — do not let it lapse.
- ICA on file via DocHub. Contact Michelle if any contractor paperwork needs updating.

## Knowledge Checkpoint

These are the questions a guide must answer without referring to notes. Use this for Phase 1 sign-off and as a self-quiz anytime.

**Operations**
1. What time do you pick up the van, and where?
2. What is the gas card code and which station do you use?
3. Where is the pickup location for shuttle guests?
4. How do 3rd-party (Viator) bookings differ from direct FareHarbor bookings?
5. Where is the waiver QR code located?
6. What is the kayak weight limit? Age minimum?
7. What happens if a guest under 16 wants to paddle solo?

**Safety**
8. Recite all four paddle signals.
9. A guest capsizes. Walk me through your response.
10. Lightning appears on the horizon during the tour. What do you do?
11. You realize one guest is unaccounted for at a content stop. What do you do?
12. PFD rule: when are they required?

**Ecology & Tour Content**
13. What are cypress knees, and what's the common misconception about them?
14. Why is the water brown? Is it safe?
15. What happened to the original cypress trees at Manchac?
16. What does Louisiana's coastal wetland loss look like in practical terms?
17. How do you estimate an alligator's body length from a distance?
18. Why don't we bait alligators, and how does that differ from motorized swamp tours?
19. What is water hyacinth and what is its seasonal effect on the tour?
20. Name five bird species guests might see at Manchac.

**Guest Situations**
21. A guest asks if they're guaranteed to see an alligator. What do you say?
22. A guest wants to feed a turtle or touch a water snake. How do you handle it?
23. A guest refuses to wear their PFD. What do you do?
24. Someone in the group is visibly scared and doesn't want to get in a kayak. What's your approach?
25. A guest asks about tipping at the end. What's the NOLA context?
