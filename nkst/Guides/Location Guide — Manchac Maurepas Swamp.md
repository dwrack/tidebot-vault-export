# Location Guide — Manchac / Maurepas Swamp
*New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours | Guide Training Reference*
*Last updated: March 2026*

> This document covers everything specific to operating at the Manchac / Maurepas Swamp WMA launch. Read the Guide SOP Master first — this doc covers only what is different or location-specific here.

---

## Overview

Manchac is our primary and most frequently used tour location. It sits within the Maurepas Swamp Wildlife Management Area near LaPlace, Louisiana — the second largest bald cypress swamp in the United States. Most new guides will train here first. The route is well-established, the launch is manageable with practice, and the ecology content is rich.

---

## Guest Pickup Location

**740 N. Rampart St. (Rampart Treehouse)** — all Manchac tours pick up here. Third-party bookings (Viator, Expedia, TripAdvisor) always include shuttle service and pick up here as well.

---

## Driving to the Launch

**Drive time:** Approximately 30–40 minutes from pickup under normal conditions.

There is a **scheduled gas station stop en route** — this is the designated restroom break for Manchac tours. Plan for it; do not skip it.

**During the drive:** Use the time to introduce yourself, preview the tour, and begin building the ecology story. By the time guests can see cypress trees along the highway, they should already be curious and engaged. See the Tour Script for van talking points.

---

## Gas Station Stop (Restroom Break)

The gas station stop en route is the **primary bathroom opportunity** for Manchac tours. There are no public restrooms at the WMA launch itself.

- Tell guests as they load at pickup: *"The drive is about 30–40 minutes. We'll stop for restrooms on the way — that's your chance to grab water or snacks too."*
- At the stop: *"We've got about 10 minutes — bathrooms are inside, grab anything you need."*
- Regroup everyone before returning to the van. Do a headcount.
- This is also when you fill the gas tank if needed. Shell card, code 6006. Save all receipts.

**If a guest needs a restroom after the gas station stop:** The nearest option is back at that gas station. There are no facilities at the WMA launch. Set this expectation before departure.

## Equipment Storage

**Trailer:** Stored at the gas station near the launch — not at 1407 Piety St. Confirm trailer location with management before your first solo tour here. The launch and trailer location should be familiar from your shadow shifts; do not attempt to back a trailer at an unfamiliar location for the first time on a solo tour.

**Kayaks and gear:** Unloaded from the trailer at the launch. Stage kayaks and paddles on the grass — this should take no more than 15 minutes.

**PFD storage and care:** Hang all PFDs on the clothesline to dry after every tour. Spray with MiraZyme before the next use. PFDs must not smell foul — check before fitting any guest.

**Cleaning:** Pressure wash equipment on Tues/Wed. After each tour, flip boats, remove trash and debris, drain excess water.

## Launch Location

**Address:** Maurepas Swamp WMA, near LaPlace, LA 70068
**GPS Plus Code:** 5H64+W3, LaPlace, LA 70068

**Parking and arrival:**
- Guest parking: direct guests to the correct lot — they do **not** park in the motorized boat area
- You back the trailer across the street to the launch. Do this slowly and carefully. This takes practice — if you haven't backed a trailer here under supervision, do not attempt it solo
- Unload kayaks onto the grass near the water's edge
- Gear up and brief guests at the launch (not in the parking lot)

---

## Launch Procedure

1. Back trailer into position at launch (slowly — practice this on shadow shifts)
2. Unload kayaks, paddles, and gear onto the grass (max 15 minutes)
3. Check PFDs for condition and odor
4. Fit guests with PFDs
5. Show guests on the map: route, regroup spots, two content stops
6. Deliver safety briefing (SOP Master, section 4.5)
7. Paddle demo and signals (SOP Master, section 4.6)
8. Assign boats (lead/sweep, tandems, singles)
9. Launch in order — tell guests to stay along shore and wait for the full group before paddling out

## Location-Specific Talking Points

Manchac has its own distinct history and ecology from Honey Island. Key content specific to this location:

**Logging history (primary content stop):** Manchac was completely clear-cut in the early 20th century. Every cypress tree you paddle through is regrowth — there is no old-growth left here. This is a sobering fact, but also a conservation arc: the swamp came back. That's both the lesson and the hope.

**"Second largest bald cypress swamp in the United States"** — lead with this on the van. It lands well. People don't expect to be paddling through something that significant.

**Choctaw heritage:** "Manchac" is a Choctaw word meaning "rear entrance" or "back way in." "Bayou" is also Choctaw. The Choctaw people navigated and traded this entire landscape for thousands of years before European contact. The language they left behind is still here.

**Water hyacinth:** The invasive purple-flowered plant covering sections of the bayou was introduced in New Orleans at a trade exposition in the 1800s. It spreads aggressively, blocks light, depletes oxygen, and disrupts the food web. In winter it dies back, opening deeper channels. A useful tie-in to how even a small human decision (putting a pretty plant in a pond) can have massive unintended consequences.

**Coastal land loss:** Louisiana has 40% of all US coastal wetlands. It's losing a football field per hour. The levee system that protects New Orleans from flooding also cuts off the sediment supply that builds these wetlands. Manchac is part of that story.

**Bull tongue:** The arrowhead-shaped emergent plant; edible starchy root traded between Native Americans and French settlers.
