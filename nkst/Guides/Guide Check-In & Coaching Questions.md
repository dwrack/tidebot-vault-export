# Guide Check-In & Coaching Questions

**Last Updated:** April 2026

> The goal here is not reporting. It's relationship. Guides receive one rotating question per week -- something genuinely curious, not administrative. Over time, their answers build a picture of tour quality, guest experience, and what each guide needs to grow. Manager uses the responses as conversation starters, not performance data.

---

## The Approach

One question per week, sent via GHL on a rotating basis. The question changes each week so it never feels like the same form. Tone is warm and conversational -- we're asking because we care, not because we're tracking.

Guides can respond by text. No format required. A few words is fine. The response goes to the manager's inbox. Claude reviews weekly and surfaces anything worth a coaching conversation.

This is not a vehicle or compliance check (those are separate). This is purely about growth, connection, and making the tour better.

---

## Rotating Question Bank

GHL cycles through these in order, one per week. Add new questions as the season develops.

### Guest Experience
1. What's one thing a guest said or asked today that surprised you?
2. Was there a moment today where you felt the group really lock in? What were you doing when it happened?
3. Did anything come up today that you weren't sure how to handle?
4. What question did a guest ask that you didn't have a great answer for?
5. If you could change one thing about today's tour experience for guests, what would it be?

### Wildlife & Environment
6. What did you see out there today that was worth talking about?
7. Any unusual wildlife, water conditions, or environmental observations this week?
8. What's one thing you've noticed in the swamp lately that guests seem most interested in?
9. What's something about the bayou you wish more guests knew before they arrived?

### Tour Craft
10. What part of your tour felt the most natural today?
11. Is there a story or piece of local history you've been telling lately that's landing really well?
12. What's something you've been wanting to add to your tour but haven't yet?
13. How did today's group energy feel compared to a typical tour? What do you think made the difference?

### Personal
14. How are you doing? Anything we can do to support you this season?
15. Is there any gear, information, or prep that would make your tours easier?
16. What's the most fun you've had on a tour recently?

---

## How Manager Uses the Responses

- Read every reply -- even a short one tells you something about where a guide is
- When a theme appears across multiple guides (same question came up, same guest hesitation), bring it to a group skills session
- When a guide mentions something they didn't know how to handle -- that's a coaching moment, not a correction. Ask about it in the next check-in or a quick call
- If a guide doesn't reply for two weeks running, reach out personally. Silence usually means something

**Claude's role:** At the end of each week, Claude reviews that week's replies and generates a brief summary for the manager:
- Any guides who didn't respond
- Any recurring themes across replies
- Any specific coaching moments worth following up on
- Any field observations worth turning into content (blog, social, tour script addition)

---

## What This Is Not

This is not a performance review. Guides should never feel like their answers are being scored. The questions are genuinely curious -- answer or don't, short or long, anything is fine.

If a guide raises something serious (guest safety concern, equipment failure, interpersonal issue), handle it directly and outside this channel. Don't let the weekly question become the place where real problems get buried in casual format.

---

## Sample First Message (When Launching)

> "Hey [Name] -- we're starting something new this season. Every week I'm going to send you one quick question, just to stay connected and hear what's actually happening out on the water. No right answers, no forms. Just curious what you're seeing. First one: what's one thing a guest said or asked recently that surprised you? Respond whenever."
