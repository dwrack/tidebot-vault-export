# Location Guide — Bayou Bienvenue
*New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours | Guide Training Reference*
*Last updated: March 2026*

> Bayou Bienvenue operates differently from Honey Island and Manchac. There is no shuttle van and no guided paddle — this is a **self-guided rental operation**. Your role here is to set up equipment, check guests in, deliver a launch orientation, and be available by phone during the rental window. Read this doc carefully — the procedures here are distinct from the standard tour format.

---

## Overview

Bayou Bienvenue is an urban cypress swamp on the eastern edge of New Orleans, accessible via the NOLA Party Barge marina on Paris Rd. It's a quieter, more self-directed experience than the guided swamp tours. Guests paddle independently on a 2-hour suggested route using our sea kayaks. The environment is beautiful and ecologically interesting — an underrated corner of the city that most guests discover for the first time here.

This location has no guide in the boat with guests. Your responsibility is setup, guest orientation, safety compliance, and on-call availability during the rental.

## Equipment at This Location

Bayou Bienvenue has its own dedicated equipment stored on site — not shared with the shuttle tour locations. Know where everything is before your first solo session.

**Kayaks:**
- Large sea kayaks stored on site
- Secured with a lock — guests receive a code in their confirmation email
- Kayak setup:
  - **Single rental:** Middle seat position; store the extra seat in an unused kayak on shore
  - **Tandem rental:** Front and back seats; heavier guest in the back
- After retrieving kayak, guests lock the kayak lock back

**PFDs:**
- Stored in a container near the launch
- Life jackets are mandatory — worn at all times on the water, no exceptions
- Check PFD condition before each session; flag any damaged or missing PFDs to management

**Paddles:**
- Stored on a paddle rack near the launch
- Guests return paddles to the rack at the end of the rental

## Guest Check-In

Unlike shuttle tours, guests at Bayou Bienvenue arrive on their own schedule within their booking window. Your process:

1. Greet guests as they arrive and confirm their reservation in FareHarbor
2. **Confirm waiver is signed.** If guests have not signed the waiver in their confirmation email, have them complete it before any equipment is touched. *Launching without a signed waiver is not permitted.*
3. Walk guests through the equipment setup:
   - Show them how to retrieve their kayak using the lock code
   - Show them how to adjust seats for single vs. tandem
   - Fit each guest with a PFD from the container; confirm correct fit
   - Show them the paddle rack
4. Deliver the launch orientation (see below)
5. Confirm they have your phone number for any issues on the water

---

## Launch Orientation

Keep this brief and practical — these guests are independent, not guided. Cover:

1. **PFDs:** Required on the water at all times. Confirm each guest knows how to adjust theirs.
2. **Launch from the marina ramp.** If the tide is low, they may need to walk the kayak into the water.
3. **Exit the marina:** Paddle **left** past the dock to get out into open water.
4. **Navigation:**
   - Follow the **line of utility poles** that run across the bayou — this is the main navigation guide
   - First landmark: a group of **shipwrecks** in the water
   - The route loops back toward the marina
   - If they get disoriented: look for the **large communication tower** and use it as a reference point; follow utility poles back; paddle toward the marina
5. **Wildlife:** Alligators are occasionally present. Keep distance, do not feed, do not approach. Same for any other wildlife.
6. **Return time:** Make sure guests know their rental end time and plan to return with enough buffer. The marina is tidal — conditions can change.
7. **Your number:** "If you have any questions, need help, or feel unsafe on the water — text or call me at [your number]. I'm here."

## During the Rental Window

You are on-call for the duration of every guest's rental. Keep your phone on and accessible. Common issues:

- Guest is lost or disoriented → walk them through the navigation landmarks by phone
- Guest has a mechanical issue (PFD strap, seat) → advise by phone or return to the launch
- Guest wants to extend their rental → contact management to authorize; do not extend on your own authority
- Guest is overdue returning → call them at 10 minutes past return time; contact management if no response within 15 minutes

## Return Procedure (Guests)

When guests return, confirm they:

1. Return the kayak to its storage position
2. Lock the kayak with the lock
3. Return the paddle to the paddle rack
4. Place the PFD back in the container
5. **Snap a photo of the returned kayak and send it to the email in their confirmation** — this keeps equipment accountability clear for the next session

## Route Reference (for guest questions)

The suggested 2-hour self-guided route:

1. Launch from marina ramp; paddle **left** past the dock
2. Head into open water — follow the utility poles
3. First landmark: shipwrecks in the water
4. Continue following the utility poles along the route
5. Route loops back toward the marina

**Navigation anchors:**
- Utility poles = main route guide
- Communication tower = visual landmark for orientation
- Marina dock = home base

## Wildlife at Bayou Bienvenue

- Great blue herons, egrets, white ibis
- Ospreys and migratory birds depending on season
- Turtles
- Gar and bass
- Occasional alligators

Brief guests on wildlife distance and no-feeding policy during orientation. This is an urban wetland — guests may be surprised at how much they see close to the city.
