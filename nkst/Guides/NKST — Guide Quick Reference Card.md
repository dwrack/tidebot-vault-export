## Paddle Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Paddle vertical, held still | Come to me / regroup |
| Paddle horizontal | Stop |
| Paddle at 45 degrees | Go that direction |
| Paddle vertical, waving | DISTRESS — need help now |

**Whistle:** 3 blasts = guest in distress or needs attention

## Kayak Rules (State Every Briefing)

- PFDs required 100% on water — USCG Type III — no exceptions
- Age 8 minimum
- Under 16 must tandem with adult
- Max 400 lbs per kayak
- Heavier paddler in the back of tandem

## Emergency Response

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| **Lightning** | Off water immediately · calm but fast · call Michelle |
| **Capsize** | Guest holds kayak + paddle · guide paddles over · assist back in |
| **Medical emergency** | Stop group · move to shore · call 911 · call Michelle · incident report |
| **Lost guest** | Whistle signals · headcount · never split group · call Michelle |

## Ecology Fast Facts

- Louisiana = 40% of US coastal wetlands; losing 1 football field/hour
- Manchac = 2nd largest bald cypress swamp in the US
- Current trees are regrowth — logged in early 1900s
- "Manchac" and "Bayou" = Choctaw words
- Cypress knees = root stabilizers (not for breathing)
- Brown water = tannins from cypress; clean; like tea
- Water hyacinth = invasive; recedes in winter = deeper access
- Alligators recovered post-1962 hunting ban — major conservation win
- We never bait wildlife — eco-tour philosophy
