# Bayou Bienvenue — Self-Guided Kayak Tour Guest Guide
*New Orleans Kayak Swamp Tours | Last updated: 2026-03-30*

> This is the guest-facing information document for self-guided kayak rentals at Bayou Bienvenue. Send to guests upon booking confirmation.

---

## Welcome

Thank you for booking a self-guided kayak adventure! We're excited for you to explore one of the most unique wetland environments near New Orleans.

This guide will help you arrive at the correct location, launch safely, and follow the suggested paddling route. **Please read through before your tour.**

---

## Arrival Location

**Address:** 2102 Paris Rd., New Orleans LA 70129
*(You can also search: NOLA Party Barge)*

**Finding us:**
- Look for NOLA Party Barge signage
- There is currently construction on Paris Rd. — you will see an opening after the construction; turn right onto a gravel road leading into our entrance
- When you turn into the marina, there is a driveway on the left side of the property
- Continue to the large open parking lot — look for the sign on the right directing you to the kayak launch
- You will see the orange and blue kayaks near the launch

**FREE PARKING available onsite.**

---

## Getting Your Kayak

Before your tour, you will receive a code to unlock the kayaks. If you do not receive a code, your kayaks will already be unlocked and ready to launch.

When you arrive:
1. Use the code to unlock your kayak
2. After removing it, **lock the kayak lock back again**
3. Near the launch area, find the container with life jackets
4. **Life jackets are mandatory — must be worn at all times on the water**
5. Grab a paddle from the paddle rack

---

## ⚠️ Waiver Requirement

**If you have not signed your waiver, check your confirmation email NOW.**

We consider launching without a signed waiver to be theft. You must sign the waiver before leaving shore.

---

## Configuring Your Kayak

Our large sea kayaks have three seat placements:
- **Single rental:** Configure for the middle seat; place the extra seat in a kayak on shore
- **Tandem rental:** Use back and front seats; heavier guest in the back

---

## Launching

Launch from the marina ramp. If the tide is low, you may need to walk it in.

Once on the water:
- Paddle **left** past the dock to exit the marina
- Continue out into the open water of Bayou Bienvenue

---

## Suggested 2-Hour Route

This self-guided route follows a natural loop through the bayou:

1. Paddle straight out from the marina into open water
2. **Follow the line of utility poles** that run across the bayou — these are your main navigation guide
3. First landmark: a group of old **shipwrecks** in the water
4. Continue following the utility poles along the route
5. The route eventually loops back toward the marina

---

## If You Get Lost

- Look for the **large communication tower** and use it as a visual landmark
- Paddle toward the marina and launch area
- Follow the utility poles back

**Need help?** Call or text: **504-571-9975**

---

## Wildlife You May See

Bayou Bienvenue is home to a wide variety of Louisiana wetland wildlife. Keep an eye out for:

- Great blue herons, egrets, ibises
- Ospreys and migratory birds
- Turtles
- Gar and bass
- Occasionally: **alligators**

**Please give all wildlife plenty of space and never feed animals.**

---

## What to Bring

- Water (stay hydrated — especially in summer)
- Sunscreen and a hat or sunglasses
- Clothes you don't mind getting wet
- Water shoes, sandals, or sneakers that can get wet
- A dry bag or waterproof pouch for your phone and valuables
- Snacks if you plan to paddle slowly and explore
- A light layer during cooler months

---

## Kayaking Tips

- Paddle at a relaxed pace and enjoy the scenery
- Be aware of wind conditions in open water — it can push you off course
- Secure your phone before taking photos
- Plan to return to the marina around the end of your rental time

---

## Returning Your Kayak

When you return from your paddle:
1. Bring your kayak back to the launch area
2. Return your paddle to the paddle rack
3. Place your life jacket back in the container
4. **Lock the kayak back with the lock**
5. **Snap a photo of your returned kayak and send to the email in your confirmation** — this helps keep equipment organized for the next guests
