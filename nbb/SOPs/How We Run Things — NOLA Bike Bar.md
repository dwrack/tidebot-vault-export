# How We Run Things
### NOLA Bike Bar

Read this before your first shift. It takes 5 minutes.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

When you're operating the bike bar, you're running the show. Route decisions, group energy, street-level problem solving: that's all you. You know the French Quarter and the neighborhoods better than anyone behind a desk.

If something on the bike bar needs maintenance, flag it same day. If a booking looks wrong, catch it before the group shows up. You're not waiting for permission to make a good call.

---

## 2. Be Real

This is a rolling party through one of the best cities in the world. People are here because they want something more fun than walking Bourbon Street. Give them that.

Be yourself. Read the group. Some want wild, some want chill, some just want good music and a cruise. Match the energy, don't force one.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

Show up on time. Show up sober. Show up ready. When you're operating a vehicle with passengers and alcohol on public roads, there's zero room for cutting corners.

Take care of the equipment. Clean up after your run. Log any issues.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If a route worked well or didn't, share it. If you got a noise complaint on a certain block, post it so the next operator knows. If you figured out a better playlist strategy for different group types, drop it in the channel.

Voice memo, text, photo. The format doesn't matter.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

You are operating a multi-passenger vehicle on public streets, often with people who have been drinking. That is serious.

Waivers signed. Head count confirmed. Safety briefing delivered. Passengers seated properly before moving. If someone is too impaired to be safe, they don't ride. Period.

Someone died on one of our boats at another location. We take safety across every business personally. Skipping a safety step is a termination conversation.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. Bring your energy and your personality. But these five aren't optional.
