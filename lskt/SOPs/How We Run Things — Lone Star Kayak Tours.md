# How We Run Things
### Lone Star Kayak Tours

Read this before your first day. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask your manager or check the Slack channel.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

You know your waterway better than anyone sitting at a desk. When you're on the water with guests, you're the one making the calls. Route changes, weather decisions, group management: that's on you.

If something at the launch site needs attention, handle it or flag it same day. If a booking looks off, say something before guests show up. If a local business wants to partner, bring the idea.

This isn't a job where you clock in and wait for instructions. You're running a piece of this business every time you paddle out.

---

## 2. Be Real

Texas hospitality is already in your DNA. Use it. Talk like a real person, not a tour script. Share what you actually know about the water, the wildlife, the area. If someone asks you something you don't know, be honest about it.

Same goes for the team. If something's not working, say it directly. Don't wait for it to become a bigger problem. We'd rather hear it early.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

Show up prepared. Follow through. Don't let things sit because the conversation is uncomfortable.

We give real grace. Everybody has rough patches. But there's a difference between a hard week and phoning it in. If you've lost the energy for this, talk to your manager. We'd rather work it out than watch you coast.

Respect also means taking care of the gear, cleaning up after your tour, and not leaving problems for the next guide.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If you found a better put-in spot, share it. If a guest had a complaint that might come up again, post it. If you figured out how to handle a group of beginners who are nervous about tipping, tell the team.

Voice memo, text, photo in the channel. Doesn't matter how. What matters is the next guide learns from your day.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

Someone died on one of our boats. That is not a hypothetical or a training exercise. It happened, and it changed everything about how we operate.

Waivers get signed on every tour. Every single one. Gear gets checked before every outing. Life jackets are inspected, not assumed. If conditions aren't right, we cancel. No debate.

Skipping a safety step for any reason is a termination conversation. Not a warning. If you see something unsafe, say it immediately. There is no version of this job where a shortcut is worth it.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. Bring your skills, your personality, your Texas charm. But these five are non-negotiable.
