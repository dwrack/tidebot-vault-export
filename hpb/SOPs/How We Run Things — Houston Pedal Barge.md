# How We Run Things
### Houston Pedal Barge

Read this before your first shift. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask your manager or check the Slack channel.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

When you're on the boat, you're the captain. You set the tone, manage the energy, and make every call on safety, weather, and passengers. Nobody in another state is going to run your shift for you.

That also means the boat is your responsibility. Maintenance issues flagged same day. Booking problems caught before guests arrive. Supplies restocked after your trip, not left for the next captain.

You own the experience from dock to dock.

---

## 2. Be Real

This is a pedal barge on the bayou, not a corporate event venue. People are here to have fun with their crew. Match their energy. Some groups want to party. Some want a chill float. Read the room.

Be the captain people remember because you were genuine, not because you followed a script. If someone asks for a recommendation after the trip, give them a real one.

Same with the team. Got feedback? Say it. Got a better idea? Bring it. Don't wait to be asked.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

Show up on time. Show up sober. Show up ready. On a boat with alcohol and paying passengers, this is non-negotiable.

We give grace for life stuff. But when you're responsible for people's safety on the water, you're dialed in. Every time. No exceptions.

Clean the boat after your trip. Log what happened. Don't leave your mess for the next captain.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If the current was weird today and you found a better route, share it. If a group had an issue that might come up again, post it. If you figured out a smoother boarding process, tell the team.

Voice memo, photo, text in the channel. Format doesn't matter. The next captain benefiting from your experience does.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

This is a vessel with paying passengers and alcohol. That combination requires your full attention every single trip.

Waivers signed before anyone boards. Head count before you leave the dock. Life jackets accessible. Weather checked. Capacity limits respected, no matter what the group says.

Someone died on one of our boats at another location. We are not exaggerating when we say safety is the job. A captain who skips a check, bends a rule, or lets a group pressure them into something unsafe is done. That's not a warning.

If you see something wrong, say it immediately.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. Bring your energy, your skills, your personality. But these five aren't up for discussion.
