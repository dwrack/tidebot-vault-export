# How We Run Things
### NOLA Party Barge

Read this before your first shift. It takes 5 minutes. If you'd rather listen, ask your manager or check the Slack channel.

---

## 1. Own Your Patch

When you're on the boat, you're the captain. Not just legally, in every way that matters. You set the tone for the trip. You manage the energy. You make the calls on weather, on passengers, on when to dock.

That also means the boat is your responsibility. If something needs maintenance, you flag it same day. If a booking looks wrong, you say something before the guests show up. If the marina crew needs backup, you step in.

Nobody from Portland is going to micromanage your shift. But that means you own the outcome.

---

## 2. Be Real

This is a party boat, not a cruise line. People are here to have a good time on the water with their friends. The vibe should feel like the best bartender you've ever had, not a corporate entertainment host.

Read the group. Some crews want to crank the music and go wild. Some want to float and chill. Match the energy, don't force one.

Same with each other. If something's not working, say it. If you've got a better idea for how to run a trip, bring it up. Don't wait for someone to ask.

---

## 3. Respect the Work

Show up on time. Show up sober. Show up ready. This one sounds obvious but on a party boat it needs to be said.

We give real grace for life stuff. But there's a line between having a tough week and not taking the job seriously. If guests are drinking and you're responsible for their safety on the water, you need to be dialed in. Every single time.

Respect also means: clean the boat after your trip. Don't leave it for the next captain. Restock what you used. Log what happened.

---

## 4. Get It Out of Your Head

If a route worked well because of current or wind, share it. If a group had a complaint that might come up again, post it. If you figured out a better way to handle a bachelor party that's getting too rowdy, write it down or speak it into the channel.

The format doesn't matter. Voice memo, text, photo. What matters is the next captain benefits from what you learned today.

---

## 5. Safety Is the Job

This is a vessel with paying passengers and alcohol. That combination demands your full attention.

Waivers signed before anyone boards. Head count before you leave the dock. Life jackets accessible and accounted for. Weather checked. Capacity limits respected, even when the group says "just one more."

A captain who skips a safety check because the group is impatient isn't being accommodating. They're being reckless. That's a termination conversation.

Someone died on one of our boats at another location. That's not a hypothetical. We take this seriously because we've lived the alternative.

---

## The Deal

These five things are the floor. Bring your personality, your energy, your style. That's what makes this fun. But these five aren't negotiable.
