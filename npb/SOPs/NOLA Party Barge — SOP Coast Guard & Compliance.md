## 📋 USCG Required Equipment (Subchapter T)

### Life-Saving Equipment
- [ ] **Life preservers** — USCG Type I, II, or III — one per person capacity
- [ ] **Throwable PFD** — Type IV — one minimum
- [ ] **Ring life buoy** — if vessel ≥ 26 ft
- [ ] **Immersion suits** — if operating in cold water (<15°C)

### Fire Safety
- [ ] **Fire extinguishers** — B-I type: 1 for each 1,000 BTU galley + bilge
- [ ] **Flammable liquid storage** — compliant containers, away from ignition

### Navigation
- [ ] **Navigation lights** — red/green sidelights + white sternlight
- [ ] **Sound-producing device** — whistle or horn
- [ ] **Anchor** — appropriate for vessel size
- [ ] **VHF marine radio** — monitored on Ch 16

### Documentation On Board
- [ ] **Certificate of Inspection (COI)** — displayed prominently
- [ ] **Certificate of Documentation** — or state registration
- [ ] **Captain's USCG license** — on captain's person
- [ ] **Stability letter** (if applicable)
- [ ] **Manning certificate**

## 🚨 Incident Report Procedure

**Any injury, near-miss, or property damage must be reported within 24 hours.**

### Immediate Steps
1. Ensure all guests and staff are safe
2. Call 911 if any injury requiring medical attention
3. Call USCG on Ch 16 or via phone if on navigable waterway
4. Do not admit liability or fault
5. Preserve scene and document with photos
6. Collect names and contact info of all witnesses

### Report Filing
7. File internal incident report (template below) within 24 hours
8. Notify insurance carrier (contact in insurance docs)
9. Notify David Rack directly: [PHONE]
10. USCG MSIS report if required (vessel accident, injury, death, >$2k damage)
