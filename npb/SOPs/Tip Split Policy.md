# Tip Split Policy — NOLA Party Barge

*Effective April 2026. Applies to all captains and deckhands.*

---

## The Split

**All tips are pooled per trip and split 70/30.**

- Captain: 70%
- Deckhand: 30%

Every trip. No exceptions. No side deals. No "I got this one, you got the next one." One system, every time.

---

## How Tips Are Collected

**One QR code per boat.** Laminated card, held up during the tip close. Points to a single Venmo or CashApp account for that boat's crew.

- Cash tips go in the same pool
- Digital tips go to the same account
- Square tap-to-pay reader available as backup if guests don't have cash or apps

**The captain is responsible for:**
1. Holding up the QR code during the close (see the tip close script in the Performance Guide)
2. Counting all tips at end of trip (cash + digital)
3. Splitting 70/30 with the deckhand before leaving for the day
4. Texting the total to JT or Jeffrey (just the number, nothing fancy)

That's it. Count it, split it, report it, move on.

---

## The Close Script (Reminder)

This is already in the Performance Guide, but here's the short version:

Last 5 minutes of the trip:

1. **Highlight reel:** Walk them through the best moments verbally
2. **Ask:** "What was your favorite part?"
3. **The ask:** "Y'all, this is how we pay our rent. If we took care of you, we'd love it if you took care of us." Hold up the QR code.
4. **Make it easy:** Don't let them walk away planning to do it later. They won't.

The difference between a $50 tip day and a $200 tip day is almost never about service quality. It's about whether you asked well and made it easy.

---

## Rules

- Tips are split the same day, every time. No IOUs.
- If there's a dispute about the count, both captain and deckhand count together.
- If a guest tips a specific crew member by name (cash handed directly to one person), it still goes in the pool. The system only works if it's consistent.
- JT has final say on any disputes. If it escalates, Jeffrey handles it.

---

## Why 70/30

The captain carries the guest experience: the welcome, the stories, the energy, the driving, the safety. The deckhand supports: cleaning, prep, assisting guests, running supplies. Both roles matter. The split reflects the weight of responsibility, not the value of the person.

If you're a deckhand who wants to earn more, become a captain. If you're a captain who wants to earn more, get better at the close.
